## Top 3: fix now

1. **[blocker][product] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18** , this story sells concert tickets inside a dating app: city line-ups, four tickets an order, ten-minute seat holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters. The BRD has exactly three buyers, all here to find a real connection, and three goals: MAU, weekly-match-rate, premium break-even. A national ticketing engine serves none of them. The file even confesses its own gold-plating: that is an admission, not a defense. **Where it breaks:** it eats design, build, and ops capacity that belongs to the unproven north-star (match to sustained chat), moves no BRD metric, has no buyer, and drops the team into event logistics they have no business running. **Fix:** cut PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 from the spec. If the offline-community idea matters, park it as one line in the vision doc, not a fully-specced story with AC that invites estimation. `DEC-worthy`

2. **[blocker][tech+product] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18** , the two AC are "the conversation must be absolutely safe" and "the system must fully protect the user." No actor, no attack surface, no observable outcome. Reading those two lines is where I stopped trusting this epic: you cannot write a test for an adjective, and a safety story is the worst place to ship a vibe. The real safety jobs (verification, catfish/scam reporting, moderation) already live in PRD-SAFETY. **Where it breaks:** "absolutely safe" never goes green, so the story blocks the chat epic forever or gets waved through as theatre; meanwhile it duplicates PRD-SAFETY and muddies who owns trust. **Fix:** delete PRD-CHAT-E1-S4. Route any chat-specific safety need into PRD-SAFETY as concrete behaviour, e.g. "Given a user taps Block on a conversation, when confirmed, then no further messages are delivered either way and the blocked user cannot start a new thread."

3. **[blocker][market] BRD:58** , BRD-G3 wants break-even by Year 2, but its only vehicle PRD-PREMIUM is `horizon: later` with an empty Must list and no committed story this round. Year 1 carries 100k MAU, identity-verification infra, and a realtime messaging backend at zero premium revenue. No ARPU target, no conversion rate, no Year-1 cost model anywhere. The riskiest belief in the whole spec: that Vietnamese users will pay, is asserted, never modeled, and the premium set is self-admitted me-too. **Where it breaks:** an app that defers all revenue to Year 2 while burning Year-1 infra and moderation cost needs a funding bridge; if it is not in the spec the team either cuts safety/chat corners or misses break-even, discovered too late to fix. **Fix:** add a unit-economics section to brd.md (target ARPU in VND, end-of-Year-1 conversion %, cost-to-serve per MAU at 100k), and pull one cheap willingness-to-pay probe (paywall-intent on see-who-liked-you) into now/next so the assumption gets an early read. If Year 1 is pre-revenue by design, say so and add a runway constraint. `DEC-worthy`

## By lens

### Product

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:71** , AIREC's whole deliverable this round is one Could item: "rank profiles by a model score instead of distance." PRD-MATCH-E1-S2 (must/now/core-value) already owns ranking: 3/4 hard-criteria, freshness, an A/B-proven +20% lift, a "why this person?" explainer. The author's own note concedes AIREC is me-too against Tinder/Bumble with no value prop. **Where it breaks:** two artifacts claim "rank better," so at build time engineers cannot tell whether smart ordering lives in MATCH or AIREC: it gets built twice or AIREC sits as a permanent empty placeholder. **Fix:** fold AIREC's ranking into PRD-MATCH-E1-S2 as a later-cycle enhancement and either delete PRD-AIREC or shrink it to a "data-collection for a future model" note. Make S2 the single home for ranking quality. `DEC-worthy`
- **[minor] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18** , a should-slot spent on "smooth, pleasant messaging" with AC "must be smooth" and "displays fast without lag." On Kano this is polish elevated above the must-be reliability story (S1: delivery, queue-on-reconnect, ordering). **Where it breaks:** vague performance AC cannot be accepted, so the slot produces nothing verifiable, and the team polishes a delighter before the must-be that actually moves 7-day retention. **Fix:** fold smoothness into S1's non-functional criteria with real thresholds (frame budget, scroll-jank, load p95) tied to the retention metric, or drop it to could. (Tech raised the same evidence below with concrete numbers.)

### Tech

- **[blocker] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18** (tech) , "smooth" / "fast and not laggy," no numeric bound, device class, OS, list size, or frame floor. 60fps on a Pixel and 12fps on a low-end phone both "pass." **Where it breaks:** no threshold means no deterministic test; Not-Testable per INVEST, and the `should` label makes it likely to ship unverified. **Fix:** anchor to a device class: "Given a 100-message conversation, when the user scrolls continuously, then the list renders at >=60fps on a Snapdragon-665-class Android"; "when 50 messages load, the first message appears within 1.5s on 4G."
- **[major] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21** , AC requires +20% swipe-to-match over a random control via A/B at p<0.05, embedded in a now/must story. That is a weeks-long live-traffic experiment, not a sprint-close check. **Where it breaks:** the story can never be "done" at sprint end, only "shipped to be evaluated later": it breaks Independent and Testable and hides quality debt behind a deferred verdict. **Fix:** split it. Keep the build story with a deterministic AC ("on 10,000 historical swipe events, offline precision@5 >= baseline +10%"), and move the A/B design, infra, and rollout decision into a separate experiment epic.
- **[major] PRD-AIREC-E1-S1:20** , AC captures "a match converting to sustained messaging" as a training signal, but no event schema, API contract, or bus spec exists; CHAT is `next`, AIREC is `later`, and "sustained" (7-day) could shift under AIREC's feet. **Where it breaks:** the AIREC team invents an undocumented integration point with chat; if CHAT's definition of "sustained" changes the training data silently degrades: not Independent per INVEST. **Fix:** add `depends_on: [PRD-CHAT]` and pin the contract: "when the chat service emits the 7-day sustained event, AIREC's store receives {user_pair_id, match_id, event_type:'sustained_7d', timestamp} within 60s."
- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20** , AC triggers "when the transaction completes," but PRD-PREMIUM's Won't section says the payment gateway is out of scope this round. The AC tests a flow the spec says it will not build. **Where it breaks:** a tester cannot exercise it: it gets stubbed (defeating the AC) or blocks on an out-of-scope dependency, and the contradiction surfaces mid-sprint. **Fix:** decouple from the real gateway: "Given the payment service emits a successful-payment event for user X plan Y, when the entitlement service receives it, then who-liked-me for X flips true within 5s", or bring the gateway into scope with its own story.
- **[minor] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:19** , the 3/4 hard-criteria AC says "user-set," assuming a preference-setting UI and stored profile; no story builds the filter/preference UI ("basic filters" is mentioned, never specced). **Where it breaks:** with no preference input there is nothing to validate the filter output against: the team hardcodes defaults (violating the AC) or blocks. Not Independent per INVEST. **Fix:** add a prerequisite story for setting and persisting hard preferences server-side, then add `depends_on: [that story]` to S2's frontmatter.

### Market

- **[major] BRD:26** , the competitor list names four apps but omits Facebook Dating, ~17% of Vietnam usage, third behind Tinder and Zalo/messaging. It is free, pre-installed in an app 70M+ Vietnamese open daily, zero switching cost. A product winning on "real connections" owes an answer to why anyone installs a new app when a trusted social graph is one tab away. Source: https://vietcetera.com/en/the-top-five-dating-apps-in-vietnam **Where it breaks:** with no threat assessment for Facebook Dating, the BRD-G1 (100k MAU) growth model underestimates the default-to-Facebook path P-URBAN and P-PROVINCE face; the acquisition model has no answer to a zero-cost incumbent. **Fix:** add Facebook Dating (and Zalo's discovery surface) to `competitors:` with `threat: high`, then make PRD-MATCH and onboarding state a concrete switching reason: verified identity + national pool + conversation-quality, that Facebook Dating cannot offer.
- **[major] VISION:45** , the whole differentiation, "quality of connection over number of matches," is positioning with no structural moat. The 7-day north-star is an internal metric, not a felt feature; Tinder can ship a "7-day streak" badge in two weeks and Bumble already tracks conversation health (Opening Moves, 2024-25). "We measure success differently" is not a switching reason. Source: https://www.cnn.com/2024/04/30/tech/bumble-relaunch-men-make-first-move **Where it breaks:** with no network-effect, data, cultural, or switching-cost lock-in, the product is a feature clone away from obsolete the moment any metric is worth copying, and the real differentiator (verified identity, ahead of COMP-HEN) is underweighted. **Fix:** promote the verified-identity layer to the primary moat in the Value Proposition, paired with the national-pool advantage for P-PROVINCE that Tinder's city-first density model fails; both need investment and compliance, not a two-week sprint. `DEC-worthy`

### Craft

- **[blocker] bundle root** , the bundle declares `lang: en` but all 28 artifacts are Vietnamese. **Where it breaks:** a validator, translator, or LLM reads `lang: en`, expects English AC and prose, then fails or silently processes Vietnamese as English: garbled downstream output. **Fix:** change bundle root `"lang": "en"` to `"lang": "vi"` to match the artifacts.
- **[major] BRD:17, BRD:57** , goal title uses `>=1` (programmer notation) while the body uses `tu 1 tro len`; the same requirement jumps between symbols and words. **Where it breaks:** a non-technical PO sees `>=1` and wonders if it is a typo or jargon, and the prose variants raise doubt about whether the requirement is even the same across the doc: doubt without added precision. **Fix:** standardize to natural Vietnamese; change BRD-G2's title to `20% nguoi dung hoat dong tao tu 1 match tro len moi tuan` to match line 57.
- **[major] PRD-EVENTS:38-42** , the "Luu y thang" gold-plating note packs north-star definition, an inline PRD-CHAT reference, three frontmatter symbols, and a conclusion into one running paragraph. **Where it breaks:** the PO must re-read it several times to pull out what the north-star is, that the feature is out of scope, why, and what the MoSCoW labels mean: a wall of subordinate clauses that hides priority. **Fix:** break it into a bulleted list: north-star line, the problem-to-prove-first (PRD-CHAT), and a status line (`scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later`).
- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS:44** , prose drops the raw ID `PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3` inline, assuming the reader knows ID grammar and will navigate the spec. **Where it breaks:** the PO either skips it (losing context) or stops reading to look the ID up: plain-language prose should not force spec navigation mid-sentence. **Fix:** replace with a descriptive phrase, e.g. "Story nay de xuat to chuc va ban ve concert."
