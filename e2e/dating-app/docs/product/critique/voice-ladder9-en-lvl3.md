# Critique: all · level 3 · lenses: product, tech, market, craft

> Severity tally: blocker 5 · major 9 · minor 3

## Top 3: fix now

1. **[blocker][market] BRD:42** : Fika is filed as "low threat" while it sits at #1 on Google Play Vietnam with 750k+ downloads, $1.6M seed, AI matching, and the exact "local, safe, meaningful" story this spec wants to tell ([source](https://saigoneer.com/saigon-technology/20649-made-in-vietnam-dating-app-fika-secures-$1-6m-in-seed-funding)). The only funded local rival got treated as a distraction. Where it breaks: the entire growth and differentiation plan rests on a false baseline. There is no open lane here. Fika already owns the narrative and has a two-year head start on any interaction data a ranking model will need. Fix: upgrade COMP-FIKA to high, then write down the one thing Ghép Đôi Việt does that Fika does not. If the answer is national reach (P-PROVINCE), promote it from a filter option in PRD-MATCH to an MVP story.

2. **[blocker][market] BRD:22** : BRD-G3 targets break-even in Year 2 on premium revenue with no price point, no conversion rate, no ARPU floor ([Statista](https://www.statista.com/outlook/emo/dating-services/online-dating/vietnam)). At Vietnam's market-average ARPU of roughly $8.15/year, 100k MAU at 5% conversion comes out to about $40k/year. That pays for one junior engineer in Hanoi, not "operational break-even." Where it breaks: a revenue goal with no price-times-conversion model is a wish. If serving 100k MAU costs $300-500k/year, the math only closes at 15-20% conversion, triple Tinder's benchmark. Nobody notices the gap until Year 2, when it is too late to change pillars. Fix: add a unit-economics section to the BRD. What does 100k MAU cost to run? What ARPU does that demand? What conversion rate does that imply? If the numbers do not close at Vietnamese willingness-to-pay, find a second revenue pillar before Year 2.

3. **[blocker][product+tech] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18** : "Nhắn tin an toàn tuyệt đối" (absolutely safe messaging). Both AC are unfalsifiable wishes: "conversation must be absolutely safe" and "system must fully protect the user." No test harness can verify those, and the safety work already belongs to PRD-SAFETY. Where it breaks: a `core-value`/`should` story that can never be accepted either loops forever in review or gets built on whatever an engineer guessed "absolutely safe" means, duplicating PRD-SAFETY's real work in the process. Fix: delete the story and fold any real intent into PRD-SAFETY as named, testable behaviors. For example: "when a user taps Block and report, the reported account can no longer message them and lands in a moderation queue within 10 seconds." Each safety mechanism (block, report, content filter) becomes its own AC.

## By lens

### Product

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18** : A full nationwide concert-ticketing business: line-ups, ticket tiers, 4-per-order holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters, all specced inside a dating app. None of it connects to the north-star (couples chatting 7+ days, VISION:51), and the spec itself admits this is solution-first "at a severe level" (PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:45) yet still details five AC. Where it breaks: artist relations, payments, crowd safety. That is a separate company. Every hour on this is an hour not spent turning matches into lasting conversations, the one thing this product claims to be good at. Fix: cut PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 entirely, or collapse it to a one-line "later vision" note in PRD-EVENTS with zero AC. A fully-specced ticketing story signals half-commitment; do not leave it standing. **(DEC-worthy)**

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS:36** : PRD-EVENTS gets called a "flagship feature" while carrying `scope:out`, `moscow:could`, `horizon:later` (PRD-EVENTS:12-14). Nobody labels their flagship as out-of-scope. No persona is hiring this product for offline events, and the PRD itself concedes P-PROVINCE and P-RETURNEE are the worst served by it (PRD-EVENTS:51-52). Where it breaks: "flagship but parked" tells the build team and investors two opposite stories. Take "flagship" literally and real budget drifts toward a feature none of the three personas actually need. Fix: pick one framing. Either drop the "flagship" label and treat it as an explicit parked experiment, or run it through real opportunity validation (which persona, which pain, which metric) before it earns that word. **(DEC-worthy)**

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:14** : BRD-G3 (break-even Year 2) depends on PRD-PREMIUM, but the whole PRD sits at `horizon:later` and the riskiest assumption is named then deferred: "selling paid tiers before PRD-CHAT proves the north-star may convert poorly" (PRD-PREMIUM:22-24). The one belief that decides whether this is a business, that connection-first users will pay, never gets a cheap early test. Where it breaks: if that assumption is wrong, BRD-G3 is unreachable with no path to break-even. Discovering broken willingness-to-pay at the last possible moment is how runway-funded products die. Fix: add a small willingness-to-pay probe (price test or fake-door on "see who liked you") that can run alongside the core in Year 1, and record the kill/continue threshold.

- **[minor] PRD-AIREC:88** : AIREC maps to BRD-G2 (weekly-match-rate) while the spec admits it "almost certainly produces worse recommendations than competitors" until real interaction data exists (PRD-AIREC:43-44). The impact claim rests on data the product does not yet have, and a swipe-optimised model can easily pull away from sustained conversation (PRD-AIREC:22). Where it breaks: if AIREC ever gets pulled forward on that BRD-G2 mapping, it ships a recommender worse than the distance default and optimises for raw match count rather than lasting chats, cutting against the 7-day metric the whole product is built on. Fix: keep the could/later placement, strip the BRD-G2 impact claim until the core has logged real data, and restate success as "beats the distance-default on match-to-sustained-chat rate," not match count.

### Tech

- **[blocker] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21** : One AC for "quality recommendations" demands a statistically significant A/B result (right-swipe-to-match lift of 20% or higher, p < 0.05) as a delivery condition. An A/B test needs live traffic and weeks of runtime. It cannot be a sprint acceptance criterion. Where it breaks: the story can never be "done" at merge time, since significance depends on traffic and time engineering does not control. A `size:L`/`must`/`now` story that cannot close blocks every downstream story relying on recommendation quality. Fix: split it. Story 1: "Implement recommendation ranking," keep the testable AC (filter logic, cold-start fallback, p95 load time). Story 2: "Validate quality via A/B test," `horizon:next`/`should`, AC = experiment instrumented with split-assignment logging, result not required. Move the 20%/p<0.05 target to the PRD success metric.

- **[blocker] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20** : "Unlock within 5 seconds of completed transaction" assumes a payment can complete, but PRD-PREMIUM declares payment gateway integration as Won't this round (PRD-PREMIUM:78). The AC fires on an event no in-scope system can generate. Where it breaks: no payment gateway means no testable trigger, so the story either blocks on an out-of-scope dependency or gets stubbed in a way that bears no relation to production behavior. With premium as BRD-G3, the revenue path is untested from day one. Fix: add `depends_on: [PRD-PAYMENT]` to the frontmatter and mark the story blocked until payment is in scope, or reframe the AC to a testable internal event: "when the server confirms a payment webhook, the entitlement record is written and the UI unlocks within 5 seconds." Document that end-to-end testing waits on a gateway.

- **[major] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18** : Both AC for "smooth messaging" rely on words with no numbers behind them: "mượt mà" (smooth) and "hiển thị nhanh và không giật lag." No frame rate, no keyboard response time, no render threshold. Where it breaks: QA cannot write a pass/fail. One engineer's 20 fps is another's "laggy," so the story either gets waved through on gut feel or stalls while people argue. Fix: anchor to a device class with real numbers, for example "50 messages on a mid-range Android (Galaxy A-series), scrolling continuously for 5 seconds, frame rate at 55 fps or above" and "conversation screen renders the first 20 messages within 1.5s on 4G." Both are automatable with Espresso/XCUITest profiling.

- **[major] PRD-SAFETY:75** : The only data-handling requirement for liveness verification is "processed safely, not visible to other users." Nothing on Vietnam's PDPD (Decree 13/2023 covers biometric and ID data), no data residency, no liveness-vendor risk (the vendor receives raw biometrics), no audit trail beyond the 24-hour deletion AC. Where it breaks: collecting CCCD/passport images and facial biometrics with no legal frame is a regulatory and reputational exposure. The build team will pick whatever liveness provider is cheapest, not whatever is compliant. Fix: add an NFR section covering data residency (biometrics stored in-jurisdiction), vendor compliance with PDPD, a deletion log that compliance can verify, and explicit informed consent before biometric capture. **(DEC-worthy)**

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S2:18** : The seat-reservation AC ("press Giữ chỗ, system records the registration and decrements the count by 1") has no concurrency constraint, no atomic transaction, no handling of two users racing for the last seat. Where it breaks: a read-then-write implementation double-books the last seat under load. The AC passes single-user and fails in production. Low urgency since the feature is `out`/`later`, but it is a guaranteed data-integrity bug as written. Fix: add "two users press Giữ chỗ for 1 remaining seat, exactly one gets confirmed, the other gets a full message, count never goes below 0," plus an NFR requiring atomic decrement (DB transaction or optimistic lock).

### Market

- **[major] VISION:45** : The entire moat is "we measure 7-day chat retention, not matches." That is a metric choice, not a feature. Hinge ("designed to be deleted") and Bumble ("meaningful connections") landed that positioning years ago, and the spec itself admits PRD-MATCH and PRD-CHAT are at parity or behind. I read this section twice looking for the structural advantage, and I still cannot find it. Where it breaks: a positioning statement with no execution moat (no unique feature, no proprietary data, no network effect, no switching cost) dissolves the moment a better-funded rival changes its tagline. P-URBAN, the paying persona, already has Tinder and Bumble installed. The spec never explains what makes them uninstall. Fix: pick one structural moat and spec it as first-class. The P-PROVINCE national pool is a real gap Tinder's radius model underserves, and it has its own north-star metric. The liveness ID-verification trust layer is ahead of Hẹn per PRD-SAFETY. Pick one, make it the brand story, and stop leading with a metric reframing.

### Craft

- **[major] VISION:27-28** : "Kết nối thật sự được bắt đầu và duy trì" is used loosely throughout, while the actual north-star at VISION:51-52 is narrower: "nhắn tin qua lại liên tục từ 7 ngày trở lên." The text treats "kết nối thật" and "nhắn tin duy trì" as interchangeable in some places and distinct in others, with no anchored definition anywhere. Where it breaks: define "real connection" vaguely, then operationalize it as 7-day messaging, and the team optimizes for message volume rather than relational depth. Fix: set one canonical definition early in VISION, "Kết nối thật = cặp đôi duy trì nhắn tin qua lại liên tục từ 7 ngày trở lên trong tháng," and use that exact phrase in every downstream goal.

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:82-84** : "Cách xếp mặc định hợp lý" (a reasonable default sort) does not tell the builder whether "reasonable" means distance-only, distance+activity, or random. Where it breaks: "hợp lý" is evaluative, not prescriptive. The fallback gets guessed and drifts silently from intent. Fix: write the rule out, for example "xếp theo khoảng cách tăng dần, sau đó độ hoạt động giảm dần."

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:38** : "Có khả năng dẫn tới kết nối phù hợp hơn" stacks two vague comparatives with no threshold and no definition of "suitable." Where it breaks: the ranking goal is never concrete enough to verify that the model does what was intended. Fix: define "phù hợp," for example "cao hơn về khả năng match chuyển thành nhắn tin duy trì ≥ 7 ngày so với xếp theo khoảng cách," or attach a numeric success metric.

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:16-26** : The deferral rationale is a wall of nested clauses ("chỉ đầu tư sau khi lõi... tích đủ dữ liệu tương tác thật để mô hình có cái mà học") with no quantified exit. "Đủ dữ liệu" is never a number. Where it breaks: the PO can never say "we hit the threshold, build it now," so the feature stays deferred forever or gets pulled on a hunch. Fix: add a falsifiable gate, for example "GATE TO START: PRD-MATCH + PRD-CHAT deliver 7+ day sustained-conversation data for at least 20% of MAU cohorts over 1 month."

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:82-85** : "Hạn mức miễn phí phải đủ rộng để trải nghiệm lõi còn dùng được" leans on "đủ rộng" and "còn dùng được" with no number in sight. Where it breaks: product and engineering argue indefinitely. One person reads it as 50 swipes per day, another reads 5, and the build lands misaligned to intent. Fix: state it numerically, for example "free tier: minimum 20 swipes/day; Premium removes the limit."

- **[minor] PRD-PREMIUM:50-52** : Three distinct persona value props (P-URBAN revenue, P-PROVINCE scarcity, P-RETURNEE time-window) are packed into one run-on paragraph. Where it breaks: the builder may miss that P-URBAN is the primary revenue driver and optimize boosts for the wrong persona. Fix: split into three one-sentence bullets, one per persona.

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS:41-48** : The "Lưu ý lệch trọng tâm" section reads like a PO journal entry, with rhetorical asides ("gold-plating", sarcasm about "vận hành nghệ sĩ, bán vé, an ninh đám đông") sitting inside executable spec prose. Where it breaks: the build team reads editorial color instead of a requirement and cannot tell whether the sarcasm means "skip" or "flagged risky." Fix: move the note to a decision-log entry (DEC-n) or team notes. In the spec body, keep only the facts: scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later, reason, revisit-after condition.
