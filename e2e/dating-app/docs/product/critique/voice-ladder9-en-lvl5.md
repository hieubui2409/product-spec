# Critique: all · level 5 · lenses: product, tech, market, craft

> Severity tally: blocker 5 · major 8 · minor 3

## Top 3: fix now

1. **[blocker][market] BRD:42** Fika is filed as a low-threat afterthought, but it is the #1 dating app on Google Play Vietnam: 750k+ downloads, $1.6M seed, AI matching, female-safety positioning, Vietnamese cultural framing. Everything Ghép Đôi Việt claims about itself, Fika already claims, with a two-year head start. **Where it dies:** the entire differentiation and growth plan rests on a false baseline. You are not entering an open lane. You are entering a lane Fika already owns, with a data lead you cannot close from a standing start. **Fix it properly:** raise COMP-FIKA to high threat, and write a competitive section that names exactly what you do that Fika does not. If the honest answer is the national P-PROVINCE pool, pull it out of the filter buried in PRD-MATCH and make it a first-class MVP story. (source: saigoneer.com/saigon-technology/20649) `DEC-worthy`

2. **[blocker][market] BRD:22** BRD-G3 promises break-even on premium by Year 2 with no price, no conversion rate, no ARPU floor behind it. Run the arithmetic the spec refuses to: Vietnam's whole online-dating market is roughly USD 6.4M/year, average ARPU roughly USD 8.15/year. 100k MAU at 5% premium conversion is about USD 40k a year. That pays one junior engineer in Hanoi, not "operational break-even." **Where it dies:** a revenue goal with no price-times-conversion model is a wish wearing a goal's clothes. If serving 100k MAU costs USD 300-500k/year (infra + moderation + ID-verification ops), the math only closes at 15-20% conversion, triple Tinder's benchmark, and nobody will admit that until it is already too late. **Fix it properly:** add a unit-economics block to the BRD: estimated operating cost at 100k MAU, the ARPU needed to cover it, the conversion that implies. If it does not close at Vietnamese willingness-to-pay, the model needs a second pillar (ads, tickets, B2B) before Year 2. (source: statista.com/outlook/emo/dating-services/online-dating/vietnam) `DEC-worthy`

3. **[blocker][tech, product] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18** "Nhắn tin an toàn tuyệt đối." Both AC are "the conversation must be absolutely safe" and "the system must fully protect the user." Two lenses stopped at the same line: there is no observable outcome an engineer can test, and no real job underneath it. The safety work already lives in PRD-SAFETY (verification + scam reporting). This is a hollow story bolted on top of a real one, carrying scope:core-value, moscow:should. **Where it dies:** a should-priority story whose done-condition is "absolutely safe" can never be accepted, never sized, never closed. The build team either ignores it or gold-plates an undefined surface that already overlaps PRD-SAFETY. **Fix it properly:** delete PRD-CHAT-E1-S4. Fold any real intent into PRD-SAFETY as concrete, testable behaviors, e.g. "when a user taps Block and report, that account can no longer message them and lands in a moderation queue within 10 seconds." Each safety mechanism gets its own named AC.

## By lens

### Product

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18** This story specs a nationwide concert-ticketing business inside a dating app: line-ups, ticket tiers, 4-per-order holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters, none of which touches the north-star of couples who keep chatting 7+ days (VISION:51). The spec even admits it is solution-first "at a more severe level" (PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:45) and then writes five concrete AC for it anyway. **Where it dies:** event operations (artist relations, payments, crowd safety) is a separate company. Every hour here is an hour not spent turning matches into lasting conversations, the one thing this product claims to do better than anyone. **Fix it properly:** cut PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 entirely, or collapse it to a one-line "later vision" note in PRD-EVENTS with zero AC. A fully-specced ticketing story in the backlog signals a half-commitment nobody made. `DEC-worthy`

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS:36** PRD-EVENTS is branded a "flagship feature" while carrying scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later (PRD-EVENTS:12-14). Nobody flags their flagship as out-of-scope. No persona (P-URBAN, P-PROVINCE, P-RETURNEE) is hiring this product for offline events, and the PRD itself concedes the two worst-served are P-PROVINCE and P-RETURNEE (PRD-EVENTS:51-52). **Where it dies:** "flagship" and "out-of-scope" tell the build team and investors two opposite stories. Take "flagship" literally and budget drifts toward a feature serving none of the three personas, away from the trust+chat core the BRD calls the prerequisite for growth. **Fix it properly:** pick one framing. Drop the "flagship" language and keep it an explicit parked experiment, or run it through real opportunity-validation (which persona, which pain, which metric beyond an indirect MAU proxy) before it earns the word. `DEC-worthy`

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:14** BRD-G3 (premium breaks even by Year 2) is the goal this PRD serves, yet the whole PRD is horizon:later and the riskiest belief is named then shelved: "selling paid tiers before PRD-CHAT proves the north-star may convert poorly" (PRD-PREMIUM:22-24). The single assumption that decides whether this is a business (that connection-first users will pay for boosts and see-who-liked-you) never gets a cheap early test. **Where it dies:** if that belief is wrong, BRD-G3 is unreachable and there is no path to break-even, but you only find out in Year 2 because monetization is fully parked behind the core. Finding a broken willingness-to-pay at the last possible moment is how runway-funded products die. **Fix it properly:** run a small early probe (price test or a fake-door on "see who liked you") alongside the core in Year 1. No full premium build. Record the kill/continue threshold up front.

- **[minor] PRD-AIREC:88** PRD-AIREC maps itself to BRD-G2 (weekly-match-rate) while admitting it "almost certainly produces worse recommendations than competitors" until real interaction data exists (PRD-AIREC:43-44). The impact claim rests on data the product does not have. A swipe-optimised model can also pull away from sustained conversation (PRD-AIREC:22). **Where it dies:** if AIREC ever gets pulled forward on that BRD-G2 mapping, it ships a recommender worse than the distance-default and optimises for raw matches instead of lasting chats, undermining the one metric the product is built around. **Fix it properly:** keep the could/later placement but strip the BRD-G2 impact claim until the core logs real data, and restate its success criterion as "beats the distance-default on match-to-sustained-chat rate," not match count.

### Tech

- **[blocker] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21** One AC makes a multi-week A/B result (right-swipe-to-match lift of 20%, p < 0.05) a delivery condition on a size:L, moscow:must, horizon:now story. **Where it dies:** A/B significance needs live traffic and weeks of runtime, neither of which engineering controls at merge. The story can never formally close, and it blocks everything downstream that depends on recommendation quality. **Fix it properly:** split it. Story 1: "Implement recommendation ranking," keep the already-testable AC (filter logic, cold-start fallback, p95 load). Story 2: "Validate quality via A/B," horizon:next, moscow:should, AC being that the experiment is instrumented with split-assignment logging (not the result proven). Move the 20%/p<0.05 target to the PRD success metric.

- **[blocker] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20** The "unlock within 5 seconds of completed transaction" AC fires on "giao dịch hoàn tất," but payment-gateway integration is declared Won't this round (PRD-PREMIUM:78). The AC references an event no in-scope system can generate. **Where it dies:** with no payment gateway, there is no testable trigger. The story either blocks on an explicitly out-of-scope dependency or ships on a stub that cannot mirror production, leaving the BRD-G3 revenue path untested from day one. **Fix it properly:** add `depends_on: [PRD-PAYMENT]` to PRD-PREMIUM and mark the story blocked, or reframe the AC against a testable internal event ("when the server confirms a payment webhook, the entitlement record is written and the UI unlocks within 5s") and document that it cannot be tested end-to-end until a gateway exists. `DEC-worthy`

- **[major] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18** Both AC lean on unmeasurable words: "mượt mà" (smooth) and "hiển thị nhanh và không giật lag." No frame-rate, no render-time, no response threshold. Smooth again, and QA has nothing to measure. **Where it dies:** one engineer's 20 fps is another's "laggy," so the story gets waved through on gut feel (regressions slip in) or stalls in review while people argue about what counts as smooth. **Fix it properly:** anchor it to a device class with numbers. "50 messages on a mid-range Android, scroll 5s continuously, frame rate stays at 55 fps or above," and "the first 20 messages render within 1.5s on 4G." Automatable with Espresso/XCUITest profiling.

- **[major] PRD-SAFETY:75** The only data-handling NFR for liveness verification is "processed safely, not visible to other users." Nothing on Vietnam's PDPD (Decree 13/2023, which covers biometric and government-ID data), no data residency, no third-party vendor vetting (the vendor receives raw biometrics), no deletion audit beyond the 24-hour AC. **Where it dies:** collecting CCCD/passport images and facial biometrics with no documented legal basis is real regulatory and reputational exposure. The build team will pick a liveness vendor on price rather than compliance because the spec gives no guidance. **Fix it properly:** add an NFR section covering data residency (biometrics stored within jurisdiction), a PDPD-compliant vendor constraint, a deletion log verifiable by compliance, and explicit informed consent before capture. `DEC-worthy`

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S2:18** The seat-reservation AC ("records the registration and decrements the remaining count by 1") names no concurrency constraint and no atomicity, so it says nothing about two people grabbing the last seat at once. **Where it dies:** a naive read-then-write double-books the last seat under load. The AC passes a single-user test and fails in production. Scope:out/later makes this genuinely minor, not urgent. **Fix it properly:** add the race AC ("two users press Giữ chỗ on 1 remaining seat: exactly one confirmation, one error, count never below 0") plus an NFR requiring atomic decrement via DB transaction or optimistic locking.

### Market

- **[major] VISION:45** The whole moat is "we measure 7-day chat retention, not matches." That is a KPI choice, not a feature. Hinge shipped "designed to be deleted" years ago. Bumble runs on "meaningful connections." Nothing in the spec (not the swipe deck, not the text chat, not the badge) is architecturally harder to copy than a tagline, and the spec already concedes PRD-MATCH and PRD-CHAT are at parity or behind competitors. **Where it dies:** positioning with no execution moat (unique feature, proprietary data, network effect, switching cost) evaporates the moment a better-funded rival changes their slogan. P-URBAN already has Tinder and Bumble installed, and the spec never says what makes them uninstall. **Fix it properly:** spec one real structural moat. The P-PROVINCE national pool is genuine blue ocean that Tinder's location-radius model underserves; build it as a first-class feature with its own north-star. Or make liveness ID-verification (ahead of Hẹn per PRD-SAFETY) the primary brand story instead of the generic "real connections" line. `DEC-worthy`

### Craft

- **[major] VISION:27-28** "kết nối thật sự được bắt đầu và duy trì" is used broadly, but the north-star at VISION:51-52 is narrower: "cặp đôi duy trì nhắn tin... từ 7 ngày trở lên." Downstream, the text swaps "kết nối thật" and "nhắn tin duy trì" sometimes as synonyms, sometimes not, with no anchoring definition. **Where it dies:** when "real connection" is vague and then operationalised as "7+ days of messaging," the team optimises for message volume over relational depth, and the same term gets read three different ways downstream. **Fix it properly:** fix one canonical definition early in VISION ("Kết nối thật = cặp đôi duy trì nhắn tin qua lại liên tục từ 7 ngày trở lên trong tháng") and use that exact phrasing in every downstream goal.

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:82-84** "cách xếp mặc định hợp lý" hides what the fallback actually does. Distance-only? Distance + activity? Random? "Reasonable" is evaluative, not prescriptive. **Where it dies:** read as a requirement, "hợp lý" leaves the fallback to be guessed, and the guess drifts silently from intent. **Fix it properly:** state the rule, e.g. "xếp mặc định theo khoảng cách tăng dần, rồi theo độ hoạt động giảm dần."

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:16-26** The deferral rationale is a wall of nested clauses ending in "tích đủ dữ liệu tương tác thật" with no number, so nobody can ever say "we hit the threshold, build it now." It stays deferred forever by ambiguity. **Where it dies:** the PO has no falsifiable entry criterion, so the feature either never starts or starts on a hunch. **Fix it properly:** add a measurable gate: "GATE TO START: PRD-MATCH and PRD-CHAT deliver 7+ day sustained-conversation data for 20% or more of MAU cohorts over one month." (Related: PRD-AIREC:38's "dẫn tới kết nối phù hợp hơn" has the same unmeasured-comparative problem; tie "phù hợp" to the same 7-day metric.)

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:82-85** "Hạn mức miễn phí phải đủ rộng để trải nghiệm lõi còn dùng được" runs on two adjectives with no thresholds: "đủ rộng," "còn dùng được." Is wide 10 likes/day or 100? **Where it dies:** product and engineering argue this indefinitely, one ships 50 swipes/day, another ships 5, and the result misses the intent either way. **Fix it properly:** put a number on it. "Free tier: minimum 20 swipes/day; Premium removes the limit."

- **[minor] PRD-PREMIUM:50-52** Three distinct persona value props (P-URBAN revenue, P-PROVINCE pool-scarcity, P-RETURNEE time-window) are crammed into one run-on paragraph. **Where it dies:** the dense prose risks the builder missing that P-URBAN is the primary revenue driver and optimising boost for the wrong persona. **Fix it properly:** split into three one-sentence bullets, one per persona.

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS:41-48** The "Lưu ý lệch trọng tâm" section reads as a PO journal entry dropped into the spec: rhetorical framing ("gold-plating") and sarcasm ("vận hành nghệ sĩ, bán vé, an ninh đám đông") that belong in a meeting note. **Where it dies:** the build team reads editorial color instead of a requirement and cannot tell whether the sarcasm means "skip this" or "flagged as risky, still in." **Fix it properly:** move the note to a decision-log entry (DEC-n) and keep only the facts in the spec body ("PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 is scope:out/could/later; revisit after PRD-CHAT validates the north-star").
