# Critique: all  ·  level 1  ·  lenses: product, tech, market, craft

> Severity tally: blocker 5 · major 9 · minor 4

The spine of this spec is genuinely good. PRD-MATCH, PRD-CHAT and PRD-SAFETY are sequenced as the real path to the north-star (couples chatting 7+ days), and the author has been refreshingly honest about where they sit against competitors. The notes below are about the edges and the gaps, not the core idea, which is worth building once these are tightened.

## Top 3: worth fixing first

1. **[blocker][market] BRD:42.** Fika is rated low threat, but it is already the #1 dating app on Google Play Vietnam (750k+ downloads, $1.6M seed, AI matching, female-safety positioning, Vietnamese cultural framing). Every claim Ghép Đôi Việt makes about itself, Fika made first. Worth a look: the growth and differentiation plan rests on a false baseline; the lane you think is open is already occupied, with a two-year data head start. Maybe try: lift COMP-FIKA to high threat, then write the one section that says plainly what you do that Fika does not (e.g. national reach for P-PROVINCE), and build that into the MVP rather than burying it as a filter in PRD-MATCH.

2. **[blocker][market] BRD:22.** BRD-G3 targets break-even on premium revenue in Year 2 with no price point, no target conversion, no ARPU floor. Vietnam's online dating market is roughly USD 6.4M/year total (Statista 2025); at the ~USD 8.15/year market ARPU, 100k MAU at 5% premium conversion is roughly USD 40k/year, about one junior engineer in Hanoi. Worth a look: a revenue goal with no price × conversion model behind it gets treated as achievable right up until it visibly isn't, and the real operating cost (infra + moderation + ID-verification) likely needs conversion at 15-20%, triple Tinder's benchmark. Maybe try: add a short unit-economics section to the BRD: estimated annual operating cost at 100k MAU, the premium ARPU needed to cover it, and the conversion rate that implies. If it doesn't close at Vietnamese willingness-to-pay, add a second revenue pillar before Year 2. (Source: https://www.statista.com/outlook/emo/dating-services/online-dating/vietnam · Fika funding: https://saigoneer.com/saigon-technology/20649-made-in-vietnam-dating-app-fika-secures-$1-6m-in-seed-funding)

3. **[blocker][tech, product] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18.** "Nhắn tin an toàn tuyệt đối" carries two AC that are wishes, not criteria: "the conversation must be absolutely safe" and "the system must fully protect the user." Neither names an observable outcome a test could check, and the real safety work already lives in PRD-SAFETY (verification + scam reporting). Worth a look: a should-priority story with an unfalsifiable done-condition can never be accepted or sized, and it overlaps PRD-SAFETY, so the build team either skips it or gold-plates an undefined surface. Maybe try: delete PRD-CHAT-E1-S4 and fold any real intent into PRD-SAFETY as concrete behaviors (block + report-in-chat, abuse filter), each with a testable AC, for example: tap "Block and report" and the reporting user stops receiving messages while the account enters a moderation queue within 10 seconds.

## By lens

### Product

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18.** This story specs a full nationwide concert-ticketing business (line-ups, ticket tiers, 4-per-order holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters) inside a dating app. None of it relieves a pain or creates a gain named in VISION or BRD; the spec even admits it is solution-first at a severe level (PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:45) yet still details five AC. Worth a look: event/ticketing operations are a separate company; every hour here is an hour not spent turning matches into sustained conversations, the one thing the product claims to be better at. Maybe try: cut the story entirely, or collapse it to a one-line "later vision" note in PRD-EVENTS with zero AC, so the backlog doesn't signal a half-commitment.

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS:36.** PRD-EVENTS is called a "flagship feature" while also carrying scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later (PRD-EVENTS:12-14). Nobody flags their flagship as out-of-scope, and none of the three personas (P-URBAN, P-PROVINCE, P-RETURNEE) is hiring the product to attend offline events; the PRD concedes the two hardest-to-serve personas are worst served here (PRD-EVENTS:51-52). Worth a look: "flagship but out-of-scope" tells the build team and investors two opposite stories, and if anyone takes "flagship" literally, budget gets pulled toward a feature serving none of the named personas. Maybe try: pick one framing. Either drop "flagship" and keep it as an explicit parked experiment, or, if it is truly strategic, run it through real opportunity-validation (which persona, which pain, which metric) before it earns the word.

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:14.** BRD-G3 (premium break-even by Year 2) is the goal this PRD serves, yet the whole PRD is horizon:later and its riskiest assumption is named then deferred: that connection-first users will pay for boosts / see-who-liked-you (PRD-PREMIUM:22-24). The one belief that decides whether this is a business never gets an early cheap test. Worth a look: if that assumption is wrong, BRD-G3 is unreachable, but the spec only finds out in Year 2 because monetization sits entirely behind the core. Maybe try: add a small early willingness-to-pay probe (price test or fake-door on "see who liked you") that runs alongside the core in Year 1 without a full premium build, and record the kill/continue threshold.

- **[minor] PRD-AIREC:88.** PRD-AIREC maps to BRD-G2 (weekly-match-rate) but openly admits it will likely produce worse recommendations than competitors until enough real interaction data exists (PRD-AIREC:43-44), and a swipe-optimized model can pull away from sustained conversation (PRD-AIREC:22). Worth a look: if it ever gets pulled forward on that BRD-G2 mapping, it ships a recommender worse than the distance default and risks optimizing for raw matches over the 7-day-chat metric the product is built on. Maybe try: keep the could/later placement but strip the BRD-G2 impact claim until the core has logged real data, and restate success as "beats the distance-default on match-to-sustained-chat rate," not match count.

### Tech

- **[blocker] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21.** One AC on this size:L / must / now story requires a statistically significant A/B result (right-swipe-to-match lift >= 20%, p < 0.05) as a delivery condition. Worth a look: A/B significance depends on live traffic and weeks of runtime, neither of which engineering controls at merge time, so the story can never formally close and blocks anything downstream that depends on recommendation quality. Maybe try: split it. (1) "Implement recommendation ranking" keeps the already-testable AC (filter logic, cold-start fallback, p95 load time, lines 18-20 and 22). (2) "Validate via A/B test" at horizon:next / should, with AC that the experiment is set up and instrumented, not that the result is proven. Move the 20% / p<0.05 target to the PRD success metric.

- **[blocker] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20.** The "unlock within 5 seconds of completed transaction" AC assumes a payment can complete, but PRD-PREMIUM declares payment gateway integration as Won't this round (PRD-PREMIUM:78). The AC fires on an event nothing in scope can generate. Worth a look: with no payment gateway, engineers have no testable trigger; the story blocks on an out-of-scope dependency or ships a stub, and a revenue-critical path goes untested from day one. Maybe try: add `depends_on: [PRD-PAYMENT]` to PRD-PREMIUM frontmatter and mark this story blocked until payment is in scope, or reframe the AC to a testable internal event: "Given the server confirms a successful payment webhook, the premium entitlement is written and the UI unlocks within 5 seconds." Note explicitly it can't be tested end-to-end until a gateway lands.

- **[major] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18.** Both AC use unmeasurable words: "mượt mà" (smooth) and "hiển thị nhanh và không giật lag" (fast, no lag). No numeric threshold for frame rate, keyboard response, or render time. Worth a look: QA can't write a pass/fail test; one engineer's 20 fps is another's "laggy," so the story gets waved through by gut feel or stalls in review. Maybe try: anchor to device-class numbers, e.g. on a mid-range Android, scrolling 50 messages for 5 seconds stays at or above 55 fps; the first 20 messages render within 1.5s on 4G. Both are automatable with Espresso/XCUITest profiling.

- **[major] PRD-SAFETY:75.** The only data-handling NFR for liveness verification is "processed safely, not visible to other users." No mention of Vietnam's PDPD (Decree 13/2023 covers biometric/ID data), no data residency, no third-party liveness-vendor vetting, no deletion audit trail beyond the 24-hour AC. Worth a look: collecting CCCD/passport images and facial biometrics without a documented legal basis is real legal and reputational exposure, and the build team will pick a liveness vendor on price rather than compliance with no guidance. Maybe try: add an NFR section covering data residency, a vendor constraint (PDPD-compliant), a deletion audit log verifiable by compliance, and explicit informed consent before biometric capture.

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S2:18.** The seat-reservation AC ("press Giữ chỗ -> record registration, decrement remaining by 1") has no concurrency constraint and no handling of two simultaneous holds on the last seat. Worth a look: a read-then-write under load double-books the last seat; the AC passes single-user and fails in production. Not urgent (the feature is out/later), but worth catching now. Maybe try: add an AC for the concurrent case (two users, 1 seat: exactly one confirmation, the other gets "full," count never below 0) and an NFR requiring atomic decrement via DB transaction or optimistic locking.

### Craft

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:82-84.** "Cách xếp mặc định hợp lý" (a reasonable default ordering) hides what the fallback actually is, distance-only, distance+activity, random? Worth a look: "reasonable" is evaluative, not prescriptive, so the fallback gets guessed and can quietly drift from intent. Maybe try: name the rule, e.g. "sắp theo khoảng cách tăng dần, rồi theo độ hoạt động giảm dần."

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:38.** "Có khả năng dẫn tới kết nối phù hợp hơn" leans on two vague comparatives with no threshold or definition of "suitable." Worth a look: if "more suitable" is never operationalized, the model's training goal is a guess and you can't verify the feature works. Maybe try: define it against the north-star, e.g. "higher chance a match turns into 7+ days of sustained messaging, versus distance ordering," or link to a numbered success metric.

- **[major] VISION:27-28.** "Kết nối thật sự" is used broadly, while the north-star at VISION:51-52 is narrower ("cặp đôi duy trì nhắn tin liên tục từ 7 ngày trở lên"); later text uses the two senses interchangeably. Worth a look: if "real connection" stays vague then gets operationalized as "7+ days messaging," the team may optimize for message volume over relational depth. Maybe try: set one canonical definition of "kết nối thật" early in VISION (tie it to the north-star) and reuse that exact phrasing in every downstream goal.

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:16-26.** The deferral rationale ("chỉ đầu tư sau khi lõi... tích đủ dữ liệu tương tác thật") is a nest of conditions with no quantified exit. "Đủ dữ liệu" is never a number. Worth a look: the PO can never say "we hit the threshold, build it now," so the feature stays deferred forever or gets pulled on a hunch. Maybe try: add a measurable gate, e.g. "start when PRD-MATCH + PRD-CHAT have delivered 7+ day sustained-conversation data for 20%+ of MAU cohorts over one month."

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:82-85.** "Hạn mức miễn phí phải đủ rộng... còn dùng được" leans on "đủ rộng" and "còn dùng được" with no numbers. Worth a look: product and engineering will argue forever over whether "wide enough" means 5 or 50 swipes/day, and ship something misaligned. Maybe try: put a number on it, e.g. "free tier: at least 20 swipes/day; Premium removes the limit."

- **[minor] PRD-PREMIUM:50-52.** One dense paragraph blends three distinct persona value props (P-URBAN revenue, P-PROVINCE pool scarcity, P-RETURNEE time window) with no separation. Worth a look: the build team can miss that P-URBAN is the primary revenue driver and prioritize the wrong persona's boost. Maybe try: split into three one-sentence bullets, one per persona.

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS:41-48.** The "Lưu ý lệch trọng tâm" section reads like a PO journal entry, with rhetorical framing ("gold-plating") and sarcasm ("vận hành nghệ sĩ, bán vé, an ninh đám đông") inside the executable spec. Worth a look: the build team sees editorial color instead of a requirement and can't tell whether the sarcasm means "skip it" or "flagged as risky." Maybe try: move the editorial note to a decision-log entry (a DEC) or team notes, and keep only the facts in the spec body: scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later, plus the one-line reason and a revisit trigger.

### Market

(The two market blockers lead the Top 3 above; the third market finding follows.)

- **[major] VISION:45.** The whole moat claim is "we measure 7-day chat retention, not matches." That's a metric choice, not a feature. Hinge ("designed to be deleted") and Bumble already own the "meaningful connection" narrative, and the spec concedes PRD-MATCH and PRD-CHAT are at parity or behind both incumbents. Worth a look: a positioning statement with no execution moat (unique feature, proprietary data, network effect, switching cost) evaporates the moment a better-funded rival changes its tagline; P-URBAN already has Tinder and Bumble installed, and "quality over quantity" is a slogan they both use. Maybe try: pick one structural moat and spec it first-class. The P-PROVINCE national pool is a real blue-ocean move Tinder's location-radius model underserves, give it its own north-star metric instead of leaving it a filter. Or make the liveness ID-verification (ahead of COMP-HEN per PRD-SAFETY) the primary trust story rather than the generic "real connections" framing.

## Worth recording as a decision (DEC-worthy)

- **PRD-EVENTS "flagship" vs scope:out (PRD-EVENTS:36).** Whether PRD-EVENTS is a parked experiment or a genuine strategic bet is a positioning ruling the PO should record, especially before any "flagship" language stays in an out-of-scope PRD.
- **Pull the premium willingness-to-pay test into Year 1 (PRD-PREMIUM:14, BRD:22).** Moving the riskiest revenue assumption out of horizon:later is a priority decision worth recording, and it touches BRD-G3.
- **Upgrade COMP-FIKA to high threat (BRD:42).** Reclassifying the only funded local competitor changes the competitive baseline the strategy rests on; worth a recorded ruling.
