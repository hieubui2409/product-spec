# Critique: all  ·  level 2  ·  lenses: product, tech, market, craft

> Severity tally: blocker 4 · major 11 · minor 3

## Top 3: sửa ngay

1. **[blocker][product] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18**, the spec builds concert-ticket sales into a dating app: city lineup, 4 tickets per order, 10-minute seat holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters. The BRD names three buyer personas, all there to find a real connection, and the only goals on the board are MAU, weekly-match-rate, and premium break-even. A ticketing engine moves none of those. The file's own gold-plating note admits as much. → **Toang ở đâu:** a national ticketing platform eats design, build, and ops capacity that the unproven north-star (match to sustained chat) still needs. It moves no BRD metric, has no buyer, and drops the team into event logistics they have no experience in. → **Hướng sửa:** cut PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 from the spec. Park the offline-community idea as one line in the vision doc, not a fully-specced story with AC that invites estimation.

2. **[blocker][tech+product] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18**, both AC are unfalsifiable wishes: line 18 wants the conversation "absolutely safe", line 19 wants the system to "fully protect" the user. No actor, no attack surface, no measurable outcome. The real safety work (identity verification, scam reporting, moderation) already lives in PRD-SAFETY. → **Toang ở đâu:** "absolutely safe" can never be marked done. The story either blocks the chat epic forever or gets waved through as theatre, and "given bad behavior, then system protects" passes for any implementation, including doing nothing. Security holes stay invisible because the AC never falsifies. → **Hướng sửa:** delete the story and route actual chat-safety needs into PRD-SAFETY as concrete behavior, e.g. "Given a user taps Block, when confirmed, then no further messages are delivered either way and the blocked user cannot start a new conversation." Mechanism, observable outcome, boundary. Not an adjective.

3. **[blocker][market] BRD:58**, BRD-G3 targets break-even by Year 2, but its sole vehicle PRD-PREMIUM is `horizon: later` with an empty Must list and no story this round. Year 1 builds 100k MAU, identity-verification infra, and a realtime chat backend, all at zero premium revenue. No target ARPU, no conversion rate, no Year 1 cost model appears anywhere. The break-even claim is asserted, not modeled. Industry benchmark is 5-10% MAU to paid, and the premium set is self-described me-too. → **Chết ở chỗ:** an app that defers all revenue to Year 2 while bearing Year 1 infra and moderation costs needs a funding bridge. If it is not in the spec, the team either cuts safety/chat corners to lower burn or misses break-even, with Year 2 arriving too late to course-correct. → **Hướng sửa:** add a unit-economics section to brd.md (target ARPU in VND, end-of-Year-1 conversion %, cost-to-serve per MAU at 100k). If Year 1 is pre-revenue by design, say so and add a runway constraint to Constraints. Pull one cheap willingness-to-pay probe (a paywall-intent read on see-who-liked-you) into now/next so the riskiest assumption gets tested early.

## Theo lăng kính

### Product
- **[major] PRD-AIREC:71**: AIREC's whole deliverable this round is one Could item, rank profiles by model score, but that exact capability is already promised by PRD-MATCH-E1-S2 (must/now, 3/4 hard-criteria, A/B-proven +20% lift, "why this person?" explainer). The author's own note concedes AIREC is me-too against Tinder/Bumble. → **Hỏng vì:** two artifacts now claim "rank profiles better." Engineers cannot tell whether smart ordering lives in MATCH or AIREC, so it gets built twice or AIREC becomes a permanent empty placeholder. → **Hướng sửa:** fold AIREC's ranking ambition into PRD-MATCH-E1-S2 as a later-cycle enhancement; delete PRD-AIREC or reduce it to an explicit "data-collection for a future model" note. One home for ranking.
- **[major] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18**: covered in Top 3 (product framing: a hollow JTBD overlapping PRD-SAFETY, burning a core-value chat slot on an aspiration).
- **[major] BRD-G3:58**: covered in Top 3 (product framing: the riskiest assumption, will users pay, gets zero near-term test).
- **[minor] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18**: a should-slot spent on "smooth, pleasant messaging" with AC of "must be smooth" / "displays fast without lag." On Kano this elevates polish while the must-be reliability story (S1) carries the core-value load. → **Hỏng vì:** gold-plating chat feel before "smooth" is even measurable risks polishing a delighter while reliable delivery (the actual 7-day-retention driver) waits, and a vague AC cannot be accepted. → **Hướng sửa:** fold smoothness into S1's non-functional criteria with concrete thresholds (frame time, scroll-jank budget, load p95) tied to retention, or drop to could. Do not keep a standalone should-story whose only acceptance criterion is the word "smooth."

### Tech
- **[blocker] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18**: line 18 wants scroll/typing "smooth", line 19 wants loading "fast and not laggy", pure adjectives with no numeric bound, device class, or frame floor. → **Toang ở đâu:** there is nothing to evaluate against, so the story is Not-Testable. 60fps on a Pixel 9 and 12fps on a low-end device both satisfy "smooth", and the Moscow should-label makes a quiet, unverified ship likely. → **Hướng sửa:** add device-anchored numbers, e.g. "Given a 100-message conversation, when the user scrolls continuously, then the list renders at min 60fps on a Snapdragon 665-class Android"; "when 50 messages load, the first appears within 1.5s on 4G."
- **[major] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21**: AC embeds a weeks-long live A/B outcome (swipe-to-match +20% vs control, p < 0.05) as a story-level criterion in a now/must story. That cannot be evaluated at story close. It needs accumulated live traffic and a significance test. → **Hỏng vì:** the story can only be "shipped to be evaluated later," never "done" at sprint end. That breaks the Independent and Testable axes and buries quality debt behind a deferred metric. → **Hướng sửa:** split it. Keep the build story with deterministic AC ("on 10,000 historical swipe events, the offline ranker beats distance-only precision@5 by ≥10%"), and move the A/B design, infra, and rollout decision into a separate experiment epic.
- **[major] PRD-AIREC-E1-S1:20**: AC captures "a match converting to sustained messaging" as a training signal, but that event comes from PRD-CHAT and no event schema, API contract, or bus spec exists anywhere. CHAT is `next`, AIREC is `later`, so the contract must already be stable when AIREC builds. → **Hỏng vì:** the AIREC team will have to invent an undocumented integration point. If CHAT's "sustained" (7-day) definition changes, AIREC's training data silently degrades. The hidden dependency means the story is not Independent. → **Hướng sửa:** add `depends_on: [PRD-CHAT]` and an AC pinning the contract: "when a conversation hits the 7-day threshold, the chat service emits {user_pair_id, match_id, event_type: 'sustained_7d', timestamp} to AIREC's signal store within 60s."
- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20**: AC triggers on "when the transaction completes," but PRD-PREMIUM's Won't section says payment-gateway integration is out of scope this round. The AC tests a flow the spec simultaneously says will not be built. → **Hỏng vì:** a tester cannot exercise it, no gateway is specced, so the story gets stubbed (defeating the AC) or blocked on an out-of-scope dependency. The contradiction surfaces mid-sprint. → **Hướng sửa:** either move gateway integration into scope with its own story, or decouple the AC: "Given the payment service emits a successful-payment event for user X plan Y, when the entitlement service receives it, then user X's who-liked-me flag is set true within 5s." Testable with a stubbed event; the real gateway stays separate.
- **[minor] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:19**: AC requires each suggested profile to match ≥3 of 4 "user-set" hard criteria, which assumes a preference-setting UI and stored profile, but no story builds the filter/preference UI (MATCH only mentions "basic filters"). → **Hỏng vì:** without the preference story delivered first, the filter AC has no user-side input to validate against. The team hardcodes defaults (violating the AC) or blocks, so the story is not Independent. → **Hướng sửa:** add a prerequisite story ("user sets age range, max distance, desired gender, serious-intent toggle; persisted server-side, returned in the user-profile API") and a `depends_on:` pointing at it.

### Market
- **[major] BRD:26**: the competitor list names four apps but leaves out Facebook Dating, which Vietnamese market data puts at roughly 17% of usage (third behind Tinder and Zalo/messaging). It is free, pre-installed in an app 70M+ Vietnamese open daily, zero switching cost. A "real connections" product needs to explain why users would install something new when a trusted social graph is one tab away. → **Hỏng vì:** with no threat assessment for Facebook Dating, the BRD-G1 (100k MAU) growth model does not account for how likely P-URBAN and P-PROVINCE are to default to Facebook instead, and the acquisition model has no answer to a zero-cost incumbent. → **Hướng sửa:** add Facebook Dating (and Zalo's social-discovery surface) to `competitors:` with `threat: high`, then require PRD-MATCH and onboarding to articulate a concrete switching reason (verified identity + national pool + conversation-quality metric) that Facebook Dating cannot offer. (source: https://vietcetera.com/en/the-top-five-dating-apps-in-vietnam)
- **[blocker] BRD:58**: covered in Top 3 (the unmodeled break-even goal). (source: https://www.businessofapps.com/data/dating-app-benchmarks/)
- **[major] VISION:45**: the differentiation claim, "quality of connection over number of matches," is positioning with no structural moat. The north-star (7-day sustained chat) is an internal measurement target, not a user-facing feature. Tinder can add a "7-day streak" badge in two weeks; Bumble already tracks conversation health (Opening Moves, 2024-25 rebrand). "We measure success differently" is not a switching reason. → **Hỏng vì:** with no network effect, data advantage, cultural depth, or switching cost, the product is clonable the moment any MAU metric is worth copying. The spec's real differentiator (safety verification, ahead of COMP-HEN) is underweighted against the positioning narrative. → **Hướng sửa:** make the verified-identity layer the primary moat in the Value Proposition, paired with the national-pool advantage for P-PROVINCE as the structural reason Tinder's city-first density fails that segment. Both need investment and compliance work, so neither is a two-week clone. (source: https://www.cnn.com/2024/04/30/tech/bumble-relaunch-men-make-first-move)

### Craft
- **[blocker] bundle root**: the bundle declares `lang: en` but all 28 artifacts are Vietnamese (`lang: vi`), breaking the contract that bundle language matches artifact language. → **Toang ở đâu:** a validator, translator, or LLM sees `lang: en`, expects English AC and prose, then fails or silently processes Vietnamese as English, garbling everything downstream. → **Hướng sửa:** change bundle root `"lang": "en"` to `"lang": "vi"` to match the artifacts.
- **[major] BRD:17, BRD:57**: the goal title mixes programmer notation `>=1` with Vietnamese prose, while body text uses `từ 1 trở lên`. The title `20% người dùng hoạt động tạo >=1 match mỗi tuần` jumps between symbols and words. → **Hỏng vì:** a non-technical PO sees `>=1` and wonders if it is a typo or jargon, and the prose variants raise doubt over whether the requirement is even the same across the document. → **Hướng sửa:** standardize to natural Vietnamese: change BRD-G2's title to `20% người dùng hoạt động tạo từ 1 match trở lên mỗi tuần` to match line 57.
- **[major] PRD-EVENTS:38-42**: the "Lưu ý thẳng" gold-plating paragraph packs the north-star definition, an inline PRD-CHAT reference, three frontmatter symbols (`scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later`), and a conclusion into five running lines. → **Hỏng vì:** a non-technical PO has to re-read it several times to pull out what the north-star is, that the feature is out of scope, why, and what the MoSCoW labels mean. The wall of subordinate clauses buries the priority. → **Hướng sửa:** refactor into a bulleted list, e.g. "**Why this is out of scope:** north-star is #pairs messaging ≥7 days (not events) · prove that problem first via PRD-CHAT · status scope:out / moscow:could / horizon:later."
- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS:44**: the narrative drops the raw ID `PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3` inline ("PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 đề xuất tổ chức và bán vé concert"), assuming the PO can navigate ID grammar. → **Hỏng vì:** the PO either skips the ID (losing context) or has to hunt the spec to resolve it mid-read; plain language should not force spec navigation during prose. → **Hướng sửa:** reference the story by description, e.g. "Story này đề xuất tổ chức" or "Một story cụ thể đề xuất tổ chức."
