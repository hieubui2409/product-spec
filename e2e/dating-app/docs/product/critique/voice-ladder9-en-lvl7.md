## Top 3: fix these first

1. **[blocker][tech+product] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18** "Absolutely safe messaging" has two AC that read "the conversation must be absolutely safe" and "the system must fully protect the user." I have to be blunt: those are not acceptance criteria, they are bedtime wishes. Nobody can write a test against "tuyệt đối an toàn," nobody can sign it off, and it sits on top of PRD-SAFETY which already owns the actual safety job. You shipped a `core-value / should` story that is untestable by design. **Blown apart because:** a done-condition that can never be met loops forever in review or gets gold-plated into an undefined safety surface. Either way it adds nothing PRD-SAFETY does not already do. **Rewrite it properly:** delete the story, fold any real intent into PRD-SAFETY as named behaviors with testable AC (block-and-report where sender can no longer message and the account hits the moderation queue within 10s; in-chat report; harassment-keyword hold) or drop it if PRD-SAFETY already covers it. Two lenses flagged the same line. That is not a coincidence.

2. **[blocker][market] BRD:42** Fika is rated low threat. The number-one dating app on Google Play Vietnam, 750k+ downloads, USD 1.6M seed, AI matching, female-safety positioning, Vietnamese cultural framing, and you filed it under "distraction." Every story this spec wants to tell about itself, local and safe and meaningful, Fika told first. You are building your entire differentiation strategy on a baseline that is simply wrong. **Blown apart because:** when Ghép Đôi Việt launches it does not find an open lane. It finds Fika already owning the narrative with a two-year data head start on any ML you bolt on later. A misclassified competitor poisons every downstream growth assumption. **Rewrite it properly:** upgrade COMP-FIKA to high threat, and add a competitive section that states plainly what you do that Fika does not. If the answer is national reach for P-PROVINCE, promote it to an MVP story instead of burying it as a filter option in PRD-MATCH. Source: https://saigoneer.com/saigon-technology/20649-made-in-vietnam-dating-app-fika-secures-$1-6m-in-seed-funding

3. **[blocker][market+product] BRD:22 / PRD-PREMIUM:14** BRD-G3 targets break-even in Year 2 on premium revenue, with no price point, no conversion rate, and no ARPU floor anywhere. I ran the arithmetic you skipped: Vietnam's whole online-dating market is roughly USD 6.4M/year (Statista 2025), market ARPU around USD 8.15/year, so 100k MAU at a 5% premium rate is about USD 40k a year. That pays one junior engineer in Hanoi, not "operational break-even." Meanwhile the single belief that decides whether you have a business at all, that connection-first users will actually pay for boosts and see-who-liked-you, is parked in `horizon:later`. **Blown apart because:** if real operating cost at 100k MAU runs USD 300-500k/year, the math only closes at 15-20% conversion, triple Tinder's benchmark, and you find that out in Year 2 instead of Year 1. Discovering broken willingness-to-pay at the latest possible moment is how runway-funded products die. **Rewrite it properly:** add a unit-economics section to the BRD (operating cost at 100k MAU, required ARPU, implied conversion), and run a cheap willingness-to-pay probe, a price test or a fake-door on "see who liked you," alongside the core in Year 1, with a recorded kill/continue threshold. Treat it as a riskiest-assumption test, not a Year-2 surprise.

## By lens

### Product

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18** A full nationwide concert-ticketing business sits inside a dating app: artist line-ups, ticket tiers, 4-per-order holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters. None of it touches the north-star (couples chatting 7+ days, VISION:51). The spec even confesses this is solution-first "at a more severe level" (PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:45) and then details five concrete AC for it anyway. **Blown apart because:** artist relations, payments, crowd safety, that is a separate company, and every hour here is an hour not spent turning matches into sustained conversations, the one thing you claim to be better at. **Rewrite it properly:** cut the story entirely, or collapse it to a one-line "later vision" note in PRD-EVENTS with zero AC. Do not let a fully-specced ticketing story sit in the backlog signalling half-commitment. `[DEC-worthy]`

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS:36** "Flagship feature" on a PRD that is simultaneously `scope:out`, `moscow:could`, and `horizon:later` (PRD-EVENTS:12-14). Nobody flags their flagship as out-of-scope. No persona is hiring this product for offline events; the PRD itself concedes P-PROVINCE and P-RETURNEE are the worst served by it (PRD-EVENTS:51-52). **Blown apart because:** "flagship but parked" tells the build team and investors two opposite stories. If anyone takes the word literally, engineering and field-ops budget drifts away from the trust-and-chat core. **Rewrite it properly:** pick one framing. Either drop the "flagship" language and keep this as an explicit parked experiment, or route it through a real opportunity-validation step (which persona, which pain, which metric beyond the indirect MAU proxy) before it earns the word. `[DEC-worthy]`

- **[minor] PRD-AIREC:88** AIREC maps itself to BRD-G2 (weekly-match-rate) while the spec openly admits it "almost certainly produces worse recommendations than competitors" until real interaction data exists (PRD-AIREC:43-44). The impact claim rests on data you do not have, and a swipe-optimised model can pull away from sustained conversation (PRD-AIREC:22). **Blown apart because:** if AIREC gets pulled forward on that BRD-G2 mapping, it ships a recommender worse than the distance default and optimises for raw matches instead of lasting chats, undermining the exact metric the product stands on. **Rewrite it properly:** keep could/later, strip the BRD-G2 impact claim until the core has logged real interaction data, and restate AIREC's success criterion as "beats the distance-default on match-to-sustained-chat rate," never match count.

### Tech

- **[blocker] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21** One AC requires a statistically significant A/B result (right-swipe-to-match lift >= 20%, p < 0.05) as a delivery condition. I will say it straight: an A/B test over live traffic cannot be a sprint acceptance criterion. It needs a user population, weeks of run time, and a separate analysis step, none of which engineering controls at merge. **Blown apart because:** a `size:L / must / now` story that can only close after weeks of production data blocks every downstream story depending on recommendation quality. **Rewrite it properly:** split it. (1) "Implement recommendation ranking," keeping the already-testable AC (filter logic, cold-start fallback, p95 load time). (2) "Validate recommendation quality via A/B" at `next / should`, with AC around experiment instrumentation and split-assignment logging, not a proven result. Move the 20%/p<0.05 target to the PRD success metric.

- **[blocker] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20** "Unlock within 5 seconds of completed transaction" silently assumes a payment can complete, but PRD-PREMIUM declares payment gateway Won't-this-round (line 78). The AC fires on an event no in-scope system can generate. **Blown apart because:** engineers either block on an explicitly out-of-scope dependency or build a stub that cannot reflect production, and since premium conversion is BRD-G3 you leave the revenue path untested from day one. **Rewrite it properly:** add `depends_on: [PRD-PAYMENT]` to PRD-PREMIUM and mark the story blocked until payment is in scope; or reframe the AC against a testable internal event ("server confirms a successful payment webhook, entitlement record written, UI unlocks within 5s") and document that it cannot be tested end-to-end until a gateway lands.

- **[major] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18** Both AC use unmeasurable words: "mượt mà" (smooth) and "hiển thị nhanh và không giật lag." No number for frame rate, keyboard response, or render time. You are doing this in more than one place, and at this point I question whether anyone owns the numbers on this spec. **Blown apart because:** QA cannot write pass/fail. One engineer's 20fps is another's "laggy," so the story is signed off by gut feel or stalls in argument. **Rewrite it properly:** use device-class-anchored numbers. (1) 50-message conversation on a mid-range Android (Galaxy A-series), continuous scroll 5s, frame rate >= 55fps. (2) Conversation screen loads first 20 messages within 1.5s on 4G. Both are automatable with Espresso/XCUITest profiling.

- **[major] PRD-SAFETY:75** The only data-handling NFR for liveness verification is "processed safely, not visible to other users." No PDPD Decree 13/2023 frame for biometric/ID data, no residency requirement, no third-party liveness-vendor vetting (the vendor receives raw biometrics), no deletion audit beyond the 24-hour AC. **Blown apart because:** collecting CCCD/passport images and facial biometrics with no documented legal basis is a regulatory and reputational exposure, and the build team will pick whatever liveness provider is cheapest rather than what is compliant. **Rewrite it properly:** add an NFR section covering data residency for biometrics, a vendor constraint requiring PDPD compliance, a verifiable deletion log on image purge, and explicit informed consent before capture. `[DEC-worthy]`

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S2:18** The seat-reservation AC ("press Giữ chỗ, record registration, decrement remaining count by 1") has no concurrency constraint, no atomic-transaction requirement, no last-seat race handling. **Blown apart because:** a read-then-write implementation double-books the last seat under load; it passes the single-user test and fails in production. **Rewrite it properly:** add the AC "two users press Giữ chỗ simultaneously on a 1-seat event, exactly one gets confirmation, the other gets a full message, count never goes below 0," plus an NFR requiring atomic decrement (DB transaction or optimistic locking).

### Market

- **[major] VISION:45** The whole moat is "we measure 7-day chat retention, not matches." That is a metric choice, not a feature. Hinge built "designed to be deleted" years ago; Bumble runs on meaningful-connection messaging. Nothing here, swipe deck, basic text chat, identity badge, is architecturally harder to copy than a positioning statement, and the spec itself admits PRD-MATCH and PRD-CHAT are at parity or behind. **Blown apart because:** a positioning line with no execution moat (unique feature, proprietary data, network effect, switching cost) evaporates the day a better-funded competitor changes its tagline. P-URBAN, your paying persona, already has Tinder and Bumble installed. **Rewrite it properly:** pick one structural moat and spec it explicitly. The P-PROVINCE national pool is a real blue-ocean move Tinder's radius model underserves, or the liveness ID-verification trust layer (ahead of Hẹn per PRD-SAFETY) as the primary brand story rather than the generic "real connections" framing. ### Craft

- **[major] VISION:27-28** "Kết nối thật sự được bắt đầu và duy trì" is used broadly, but the north-star at VISION:51-52 is narrower ("nhắn tin qua lại liên tục từ 7 ngày trở lên"). The text uses "kết nối thật" and "nhắn tin duy trì" sometimes interchangeably, sometimes distinctly, with no anchoring definition. **Blown apart because:** when "real connection" is vague then operationalised as "7+ days of messaging," the team optimises message volume over relational depth. **Rewrite it properly:** set one canonical definition early in VISION ("Kết nối thật = cặp đôi duy trì nhắn tin qua lại liên tục từ 7 ngày trở lên trong tháng") and use that exact phrasing in every downstream goal.

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:82-84** "Cách xếp mặc định hợp lý," "reasonable," never says whether the fallback is distance-only, distance+activity, or random. It is evaluative, not prescriptive. **Blown apart because:** the builder cannot know which sorting rule to implement, so the fallback gets guessed and silently drifts from intent. **Rewrite it properly:** state the rule. "Xếp mặc định theo khoảng cách tăng dần, sau đó theo độ hoạt động giảm dần."

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:38** "Có khả năng dẫn tới kết nối phù hợp hơn" stacks two vague comparatives with no threshold and no definition of "suitable." **Blown apart because:** the ranking goal is never operationalised, so the model-training target is a guess and you cannot verify the feature works. **Rewrite it properly:** define it against the north-star. "Match chuyển thành nhắn tin duy trì >= 7 ngày cao hơn so với xếp theo khoảng cách."

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:16-26** The deferral rationale is a wall of nested clauses jamming three conditions together, and "đủ dữ liệu tương tác thật" is never quantified. **Blown apart because:** nobody can say "we hit the threshold, build it now," so the feature stays deferred forever on vibes. **Rewrite it properly:** add a measurable gate. "GATE TO START: PRD-MATCH + PRD-CHAT deliver 7+ day sustained-conversation data for >= 20% of MAU cohorts for 1 month." A falsifiable entry criterion.

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:82-85** "Hạn mức miễn phí phải đủ rộng để trải nghiệm lõi còn dùng được." "Đủ rộng" and "còn dùng được" are adjectives with no number. **Blown apart because:** one team reads it as 50 swipes/day, another as 5, and the free tier ships misaligned to intent. **Rewrite it properly:** "Daily free swipe limit: minimum 20/day; Premium removes the cap." Measurable, reviewable.

- **[minor] PRD-PREMIUM:50-52** Three distinct persona value props (P-URBAN revenue-motivated, P-PROVINCE pool-scarcity, P-RETURNEE time-window) crammed into one run-on paragraph. **Blown apart because:** the builder may miss that P-URBAN is the primary revenue driver and optimise boost for the wrong persona. **Rewrite it properly:** split into three one-sentence bullets, one motivation each.

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS:41-48** The "Lưu ý lệch trọng tâm" section reads like a PO journal entry: rhetorical framing ("gold-plating," "solution-first ở mức nghiêm trọng") and sarcasm ("vận hành nghệ sĩ, bán vé, an ninh đám đông") inside executable spec prose. **Blown apart because:** the build team reads editorial color instead of a requirement, and the in/out scope boundary gets muddied. **Rewrite it properly:** move the editorial note to a decision log (DEC-n) or team notes; in the spec body keep only the facts (scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later, reason, revisit-after gate). `[DEC-worthy]`
