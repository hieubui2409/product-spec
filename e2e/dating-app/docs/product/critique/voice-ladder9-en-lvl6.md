## Top 3: fix these now

1. **[blocker][product] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18.** You bolted a national concert-ticketing business onto a dating app. City line-ups, four tickets an order, ten-minute seat holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters. Reading this is the moment I gave up assuming the spec had a single author who remembered what the product was for. The BRD hired exactly three people to find a real connection (VISION:51) and the only goals on the board are MAU, weekly match rate, and premium break-even (BRD-G3:56). A ticketing engine serves none of them, and you knew that, because you wrote the gold-plating confession yourself and shipped the story anyway. A footnote admitting you cut a corner is not a defense; it is a signed receipt. Why it's done for: artist ops, crowd safety, ticketing logistics, none of which this team can handle, eat the build capacity the unproven match-to-chat loop needs. So you ship a business nobody hired while the core loop stays half-finished. Just retype it: cut PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 from the spec entirely. If offline community matters, leave it as one line in the vision doc, not a fully-AC'd story begging to be estimated.

2. **[blocker][tech] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18.** Both acceptance criteria are wishes wearing a story's clothes: line 18 says the conversation must be "absolutely safe", line 19 says the system must "fully protect" the user. No actor, no attack surface, no observable outcome. You did not write acceptance criteria here; you wrote a horoscope and walked off. The laziest part is that this is the SAFETY story, the one place where hand-waving gets someone catfished. Why it's done for: nobody can write a test that asserts "absolutely safe", so "given bad behavior, then system fully protects" passes for an implementation that does literally nothing. The story ships and the security holes stay invisible because the AC can never be falsified. Just retype it: replace both with falsifiable controls. For example, "Given a message contains a phone-number pattern, when delivered, then a safety-tip overlay shows, the message is not blocked, and the event is logged for moderation"; and "Given a user taps Block, when confirmed, then no further messages from that user reach either party and they cannot start a new conversation."

3. **[blocker][market] BRD:58.** BRD-G3 promises break-even by Year 2, but the only vehicle that earns a cent, PRD-PREMIUM, is `horizon: later` with an empty Must list and not one committed story this round (PRD-PREMIUM:13). Year 1 builds 100k MAU, runs identity-verification infra, and operates a realtime chat backend on zero revenue, and the break-even number is just asserted into the spec with no ARPU, no conversion rate, no cost model behind it. You typed a financial goal and then refused to do a single line of the arithmetic that would tell you whether it is a plan or a prayer. Why it's done for: an app that defers all revenue to Year 2 while carrying Year 1 infra and moderation costs needs a funding bridge. No bridge in the spec means the team either cuts safety to lower burn or misses break-even, and the me-too premium set (PRD-PREMIUM:42 admits it) means conversion leans entirely on retention you have not built. Just retype it: add a unit-economics section to brd.md with a target ARPU in VND, a rough end-of-Year-1 conversion percentage, and cost-to-serve per MAU at 100k. If Year 1 is pre-revenue by design, say so, and add a runway constraint so PRD owners know they operate under burn.

### Tech

- **[blocker] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18.** "Smooth" scrolling, "fast and not laggy" loading. Pure adjectives, no numeric bound, no device class, no frame floor. 60fps on a Pixel and 12fps on a budget phone both "pass." Why it's done for: no threshold means no deterministic test, the story is Not-Testable per INVEST, and the "should" label makes it likely to ship unverified. Just retype it: "Given a 100-message conversation, when scrolling continuously, then the list renders at >=60fps on a Snapdragon-665-class Android"; "Given a conversation opens, when 50 messages load, then the first appears within 1.5s on 4G."

- **[major] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21.** The AC demands a +20% swipe-to-match lift over a random control, A/B-tested at p<0.05, on a `now`/`must` story. That is a weeks-long live-traffic experiment dressed up as a sprint-close criterion. Why it's done for: the story cannot be accepted at sprint end because the AC is not evaluable until days of production data exist, breaking Independent and Testable; engineering can only "ship to be evaluated later", which hides quality debt. Just retype it: split it. Keep the build story with a deterministic AC ("offline on 10k historical swipes, precision@5 beats the distance-only baseline by >=10%"), and create a separate experiment epic that owns the A/B design, infra, and rollout decision.

- **[major] PRD-AIREC-E1-S1:20.** The AC captures "a match converting to sustained messaging" as a training signal, but that event is produced by PRD-CHAT and no event schema, API contract, or bus spec exists anywhere in the bundle, with PRD-CHAT at `next` and AIREC at `later`. Why it's done for: the AIREC team will invent an undocumented integration contract, and if PRD-CHAT's "sustained" 7-day definition shifts, AIREC's training data silently rots. Not Independent per INVEST. Just retype it: add `depends_on: [PRD-CHAT]` and an AC pinning the contract: "Given a conversation hits the 7-day threshold, when the chat service emits the event, then AIREC's signal store receives {user_pair_id, match_id, event_type:'sustained_7d', timestamp} within 60s."

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20.** The AC fires "when the transaction completes", but PRD-PREMIUM's own Won't section (line 78) says payment-gateway integration is out of scope this round. The story tests a flow the spec simultaneously refuses to build. Why it's done for: a tester cannot exercise it; it gets stubbed (defeating the AC) or blocked on an out-of-scope dependency, and the contradiction surfaces mid-sprint. Just retype it: either pull payment-gateway integration into scope with its own story, or decouple the AC: "Given the payment service emits a successful-payment event for user X plan Y, when the entitlement service receives it, then the who-liked-me flag for X is true within 5s."

- **[minor] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:19.** The AC requires matching 3 of 4 "user-set" hard criteria, which silently assumes a preference-setting UI and stored profile that no story in the spec builds (PRD-MATCH's Should mentions "basic filters", nothing implements them). Why it's done for: with no preference input there is nothing to validate output against, so the team hardcodes defaults (violating the AC) or blocks. Not Independent. Just retype it: add a prerequisite story for setting and persisting hard preferences server-side, then add `depends_on: [that story id]` to S2's frontmatter.
