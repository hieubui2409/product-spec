## Top 3: fix these first

1. **[blocker][market] BRD:42**, Fika sits rated low threat while it already owns the #1 Google Play Vietnam slot, 750k+ downloads, $1.6M seed, AI matching, and the exact "local, safe, meaningful" story Ghép Đôi Việt wants to tell. Reading this I went back and re-checked the rating twice, because misclassifying your only funded local rival as a distraction is not a small slip. **Broken because:** the whole growth-and-differentiation plan rests on a false baseline. You walk in expecting an open lane and instead find Fika already telling your story with a two-year data lead for any ranking model you build later. **Fix now:** upgrade COMP-FIKA to high threat, and write the one section that says what you do that Fika does not. If the answer is national reach for P-PROVINCE, make it a first-class MVP story, not a filter buried in PRD-MATCH. (source: saigoneer.com/saigon-technology/20649-made-in-vietnam-dating-app-fika-secures-$1-6m-in-seed-funding)

2. **[blocker][market] BRD:22**, BRD-G3 wants break-even in Year 2 on premium revenue with no price point, no conversion target, no ARPU floor anywhere. I ran the arithmetic the spec refused to: at Vietnam's USD 8.15/year average ARPU, 100k MAU at 5% conversion yields about USD 40k/year, which pays one junior engineer in Hanoi, not "operational break-even." **Broken because:** a revenue goal with no price-times-conversion model is a wish, not a plan. If serving 100k MAU costs USD 300-500k/year (a fair floor for verification, moderation, and infra), the math only closes at 15-20% conversion, triple Tinder's benchmark. Nobody confronts this, so BRD-G3 reads as achievable right up until it visibly is not. **Fix now:** add a unit-economics block to the BRD: estimated annual op-cost at 100k MAU, required ARPU to cover it, the conversion that implies. If it does not close at Vietnamese willingness-to-pay, you need a second revenue pillar before Year 2. (source: statista.com/outlook/emo/dating-services/online-dating/vietnam)

3. **[blocker][tech][product] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18**, the "nhắn tin an toàn tuyệt đối" story has two AC that are pure wishes: "the conversation must be absolutely safe" and "the system must fully protect the user." Both product and tech caught this independently. There is nothing here an engineer can write a test against, and it is `scope: core-value`, `moscow: should`, so it is in scope yet impossible to close by construction. Reading an unfalsifiable AC sitting on a core-value story made me wince. **Broken because:** the build team either loops this story forever or invents its own meaning of "safe," and either way it just duplicates the real work already owned by PRD-SAFETY. **Fix now:** delete PRD-CHAT-E1-S4 and fold any genuine intent into PRD-SAFETY as concrete, named behaviors (block user, report-in-chat, harassment-keyword hold), each with its own testable Given-When-Then AC. If PRD-SAFETY already covers it, drop it outright. **(DEC-worthy)**

## By lens

### Product

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18**, this story specs a full nationwide concert-ticketing business inside a dating app: artist line-ups, ticket tiers, 4-per-order holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters, and not one piece of it relieves a pain named in VISION or BRD. The north-star is couples chatting 7+ days (VISION:51); a concert ticket touches none of it. The spec even admits this is solution-first "ở mức nghiêm trọng" (PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:45) and then writes five concrete AC for it anyway. **Broken because:** event ops (artist relations, payments, crowd safety) is a separate company. Every hour here is an hour not spent turning matches into sustained conversation, the one thing the product claims to do better. **Fix now:** cut PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 entirely, or collapse it to a one-line "later vision" note with zero AC. A fully-specced ticketing story in the backlog signals you are half-committed to a business you are not in. **(DEC-worthy)**
- **[major] PRD-EVENTS:36**, PRD-EVENTS is called a "flagship feature" (line 36) while carrying `scope:out`, `moscow:could`, `horizon:later` (lines 12-14). Nobody flags their flagship as out-of-scope, and none of the three personas (P-URBAN, P-PROVINCE, P-RETURNEE) is hiring the product for offline events; the PRD concedes the last two are the worst-served by it (lines 51-52). **Broken because:** "flagship but parked" tells the build team and investors two opposite stories. If anyone takes "flagship" literally, real budget gets pulled toward a feature serving no named persona. **Fix now:** pick one framing. Either delete the "flagship" wording and keep this as an explicit parked experiment, or run it through real opportunity validation (which persona, which pain, which metric beyond the MAU proxy) before it earns the word. **(DEC-worthy)**
- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:14**, BRD-G3 (break-even by Year 2) is the goal this PRD serves, yet the whole thing is `horizon:later` (line 14) and the riskiest belief is named then deferred: selling paid tiers before PRD-CHAT proves the north-star may convert poorly (lines 22-24). The single assumption that decides whether this is a business (that connection-first users will pay for boosts and see-who-liked-you) is never cheaply tested. **Broken because:** if that belief is wrong, BRD-G3 is unreachable and you find out in Year 2, the latest possible moment. That is exactly how runway-funded products die. **Fix now:** run a small willingness-to-pay probe (price test or fake-door on "see who liked you") alongside the core in Year 1, and record a kill/continue threshold. Treat it as a riskiest-assumption test, not a Year-2 surprise.
- **[minor] PRD-AIREC:88**, AIREC maps itself to BRD-G2 (weekly-match-rate, line 88) but the spec admits it is a me-too that "almost certainly produces worse recommendations than competitors" until real interaction data exists (lines 43-44). The BRD-G2 impact claim rests on data you do not have, and a swipe-optimized model can pull away from sustained conversation (line 22). **Broken because:** if AIREC ever gets pulled forward on that mapping, it ships a recommender worse than the distance default and optimizes for raw matches instead of lasting chat, undermining the one metric the product is built on. **Fix now:** keep the could/later slot but strip the BRD-G2 claim until the core logs real data, and restate its success as "beats the distance-default on match-to-sustained-chat rate," not match count.

### Tech

- **[blocker] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21**, one AC requires a statistically significant A/B result (right-swipe-to-match lift >= 20%, p < 0.05) as a delivery condition. An A/B test needs live traffic and weeks of run time; it cannot be a sprint acceptance criterion. **Broken because:** the story can never be "done" at merge. Significance depends on traffic and time engineering does not control, so a `size:L`, `moscow:must`, `horizon:now` story stays open for weeks and blocks everything downstream that needs recommendation quality. **Fix now:** split it. Story 1 "Implement recommendation ranking": AC covers filter logic, cold-start fallback, p95 load (lines 18-20, 22 are already testable). Story 2 "Validate via A/B": `horizon:next`/`moscow:should`, AC requires the experiment instrumented with split-assignment logging, not the result proven. Move the 20%/p<0.05 target to the PRD success metric.
- **[blocker] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20**, the "unlock within 5 seconds of completed transaction" AC assumes a payment can complete, but payment-gateway integration is declared Won't this round (PRD-PREMIUM:78). The AC references "giao dịch hoàn tất," an event no in-scope system can generate. **Broken because:** with no gateway, there is no testable trigger, so the story either blocks on an out-of-scope dependency or gets built on a stub that cannot mirror production, putting the revenue path (BRD-G3) at risk from day one. **Fix now:** add `depends_on: [PRD-PAYMENT]` to PRD-PREMIUM frontmatter and mark this story blocked until payment is in scope; or reframe the AC to a testable internal event ("given the server confirms a payment webhook, the entitlement record is written and the UI unlocks within 5s"), and document that it cannot be tested end-to-end until a gateway exists. **(DEC-worthy)**
- **[major] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18**, both AC use unmeasurable words: "mượt mà" and "hiển thị nhanh và không giật lag." No number for frame rate, keyboard response, or render time. **Broken because:** QA cannot write a pass/fail test. One engineer's 20 fps is another's "laggy," so this gets waved through by gut feel (and the regressions ride along) or stalls in review. **Fix now:** anchor to a device class: "50 messages on a mid-range Android, scroll 5s, frame rate stays >= 55 fps" and "first 20 messages render within 1.5s on 4G." Both are automatable with Espresso/XCUITest profiling.
- **[major] PRD-SAFETY:75**, the only data-handling NFR for liveness verification is "processed safely, not visible to other users." No PDPD Decree 13/2023 reference for biometric/ID data, no data residency, no third-party vendor vetting (the vendor gets raw biometrics), no audit trail beyond the 24h-deletion AC. **Broken because:** collecting CCCD/passport images and facial biometrics with no legal frame is real legal and reputational exposure, and the build team will pick whatever liveness provider is cheapest rather than compliant. **Fix now:** add an NFR section covering data residency, a PDPD-compliant vendor constraint, a deletion-log audit the compliance team can verify, and explicit informed consent before biometric capture.
- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S2:18**, the seat-reservation AC decrements the remaining count by 1 with no concurrency constraint, no atomic-transaction requirement, no handling of two simultaneous holds on the last seat. **Broken because:** without a lock or compare-and-swap, a read-then-write double-books the last seat under load. The AC passes single-user and fails in production. **Fix now:** add "two users press Giữ chỗ on the last seat, exactly one gets confirmation, the other an 'event full' error, count never goes below 0," plus an NFR requiring atomic decrement via DB transaction or optimistic locking.

### Market

- **[major] VISION:45**, the entire moat is "we measure 7-day chat retention, not matches." That is a metric choice, not a feature. Hinge built "designed to be deleted" years ago; Bumble already sells meaningful connections. Nothing here (not the swipe deck, not the text chat, not the identity badge) is architecturally harder to copy than a positioning line, and the spec itself calls PRD-MATCH/PRD-CHAT parity-or-behind. **Broken because:** a positioning statement with no execution moat (unique feature, proprietary data, network effect, switching cost) evaporates the moment a better-funded rival changes their tagline. P-URBAN already has Tinder and Bumble installed and the spec never says what makes them uninstall. **Fix now:** pick one structural moat and spec it explicitly: the P-PROVINCE national pool that Tinder's radius model underserves, or the liveness ID-verification that already sits ahead of Hẹn. Make it the primary brand story with its own north-star metric, not a footnote. **(DEC-worthy)**

### Craft

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:82-84**, "cách xếp mặc định hợp lý" hides what the fallback actually is: distance-only? distance+activity? random? **Broken because:** "hợp lý" is evaluative, not prescriptive, so the builder guesses the sort rule and the behavior silently drifts from intent. **Fix now:** state the rule: "xếp theo khoảng cách tăng dần, sau đó theo độ hoạt động giảm dần."
- **[major] PRD-AIREC:38**, "có khả năng dẫn tới kết nối phù hợp hơn" stacks two vague comparatives with no threshold or definition of "suitable." **Broken because:** "suitability" is never operationalized, so the training goal becomes a guess and you cannot verify the feature works. **Fix now:** define it against the north-star: "higher chance the match turns into sustained messaging >= 7 days, versus distance sort," or link a numeric metric.
- **[major] VISION:27-28**, "kết nối thật sự được bắt đầu và duy trì" is used broadly while the north-star at VISION:51-52 is narrower (7+ days of continuous messaging). The text uses "kết nối thật" and "nhắn tin duy trì" sometimes as synonyms, sometimes not, with no anchoring definition. **Broken because:** when "real connection" is vague then operationalized as 7-day messaging, the team may optimize message volume over relational depth. **Fix now:** set one canonical definition early in VISION: "Kết nối thật = cặp đôi duy trì nhắn tin qua lại liên tục từ 7 ngày trở lên trong tháng," and reuse that exact phrasing downstream.
- **[major] PRD-AIREC:16-26**, the deferral rationale is a wall of nested clauses smashing three conditions together with no quantified exit. "đủ dữ liệu tương tác thật" is never a number. **Broken because:** the PO can never say "we hit the threshold, build it now," so the feature stays deferred forever by default. **Fix now:** separate the concerns and add a measurable gate: "GATE TO START: PRD-MATCH + PRD-CHAT deliver 7+ day sustained-conversation data for 20%+ of MAU cohorts over 1 month."
- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:82-85**, "Hạn mức miễn phí phải đủ rộng... còn dùng được" has no threshold. "đủ rộng" could be 5 or 50 a day. **Broken because:** product and engineering argue forever over what "wide enough" means and ship something misaligned to intent. **Fix now:** state the number: "free tier: minimum 20 swipes/day; Premium removes the limit."
- **[minor] PRD-PREMIUM:50-52**, three distinct persona value props (P-URBAN revenue, P-PROVINCE pool-scarcity, P-RETURNEE time-window) are jammed into one run-on paragraph. **Broken because:** the dense prose risks the builder missing that P-URBAN is the primary revenue driver and optimizing boost for the wrong persona. **Fix now:** split into three one-sentence bullets, one motivation each.
- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS:41-48**, the "Lưu ý lệch trọng tâm" section reads as a PO journal entry with rhetorical framing and sarcasm ("vận hành nghệ sĩ, bán vé, an ninh đám đông") inside the executable spec. **Broken because:** the build team reads editorial color instead of a requirement and cannot tell if the sarcasm means "skip" or "flagged risky." **Fix now:** move the note to a decision-log entry (DEC-n) and keep only the facts in the spec body: "PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 is scope:out/could/later; revisit after PRD-CHAT validates the north-star."
