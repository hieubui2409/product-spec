# Critique: all · level 4 · lenses: product, tech, market, craft

> Severity tally: blocker 4 · major 9 · minor 3

## Top 3: fix now

1. **[blocker][product] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18.** A concert-ticketing engine has crawled into a dating app: city line-ups, four tickets per order, ten-minute seat holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters. The BRD hired exactly three buyers (P-URBAN, P-PROVINCE, P-RETURNEE) to find a sustained real connection (VISION:51), and the board carries only MAU, weekly-match-rate, and premium break-even (BRD-G3:56). Honestly, by the time I reached the QR-ticket line I had to double-check I was still reading the same spec. None of those buyers ever asked to buy a concert seat. **Broken because:** a national ticketing platform eats design, build, and ops capacity that belongs to the unproven north-star (match to sustained chat), moves no BRD metric, has no buyer, and drags the team into crowd-safety and artist-ops work they have no business doing while the core loop sits half-built. **Fix now:** cut PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 from the spec. If the offline-community dream matters, park it as a one-line idea in the vision doc, not a fully-specced story with AC that begs to be estimated.

2. **[blocker][market] BRD:58.** BRD-G3 promises break-even by Year 2, but its only delivery vehicle, PRD-PREMIUM, is `horizon: later` with an empty Must list. Year 1 builds 100k MAU, runs identity-verification infra, and operates a realtime messaging backend, all at zero premium revenue. The industry converts 5-10% of MAU to paid; at 100k that is 5,000-10,000 payers, yet there is no target ARPU, no conversion rate, no Year 1 cost model anywhere in the spec. The break-even number is asserted, not modeled. **Broken because:** an app that defers every dong of revenue to Year 2 while carrying Year 1 infra and moderation costs needs a funding bridge, and the spec has none. The team either cuts safety and chat corners to slow the burn or sails past break-even blind. **Fix now:** add a unit-economics block to brd.md with a target ARPU in VND, a rough end-of-Year-1 premium conversion %, and a cost-to-serve estimate per MAU at 100k. If Year 1 is pre-revenue by design, say so explicitly and write a runway constraint into Constraints so PRD owners know they build under burn. _(source: businessofapps.com/data/dating-app-benchmarks)_

3. **[blocker][tech, product] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18.** A safety story whose two acceptance criteria are "the conversation must be absolutely safe" and "the system must fully protect the user." That is a wish, not a criterion: no actor, no attack surface, no observable outcome. Doing literally nothing passes both. Reading it I just stopped, there is nothing here a QA gate can ever stamp. Worse, the real trust jobs (identity verification, catfish/scam reporting, moderation) already live in PRD-SAFETY. **Broken because:** an AC of "absolutely safe" never falsifies, so the story either blocks the chat epic forever or gets waved through as theatre while security holes stay invisible, and it double-claims the trust job PRD-SAFETY already owns. **Fix now:** delete PRD-CHAT-E1-S4 and route any chat-specific safety need into PRD-SAFETY as concrete behaviour, e.g. "Given a user taps Block on a thread, when confirmed, then no further messages are delivered either way and the blocked user cannot start a new conversation." Never keep a story whose acceptance is an unfalsifiable adjective.

## By lens

### Product

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:71.** AIREC's whole deliverable this round is one Could item: rank discovery profiles by a model score instead of distance-only. But MATCH already owns that. PRD-MATCH-E1-S2 is a must/now/core-value story with 3/4 hard-criteria matching, freshness filtering, an A/B-proven +20% swipe-to-match lift, and a "why this person?" explainer (PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:19). The author's own note concedes AIREC is me-too against Tinder/Bumble (PRD-AIREC:41). So AIREC is a whole PRD wrapped around a job core matching already does. **Broken because:** two artifacts now claim "rank profiles better," so engineers cannot tell whether smart ordering lives in MATCH (must/now) or AIREC (could/later), and it either gets built twice or AIREC sits as a permanent empty placeholder. **Fix now:** fold AIREC's ranking ambition into PRD-MATCH as a later-cycle enhancement of S2, then delete PRD-AIREC or shrink it to an explicit "data-collection for a future model" note. Make MATCH-E1-S2 the single home for ranking quality. _(DEC-worthy: collapsing a scope:in PRD reshapes the roadmap.)_

- **[minor] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18.** A should-slot spent on "smooth, pleasant messaging," with AC of "must be smooth" and "displays fast without lag." On Kano that is polish being elevated while the must-be reliability story (S1: delivery, queue-on-reconnect, ordering) carries the actual core-value load. **Broken because:** polishing chat feel before the spec can even define "smooth" measurably risks buffing a delighter while reliable delivery, the thing that moves 7-day retention, goes unproven. A vague AC cannot be accepted anyway. **Fix now:** fold the smoothness need into S1's non-functional criteria with real thresholds (frame budget, scroll-jank budget, load p95) tied to the retention metric, or drop it to could. Do not keep a standalone should-story whose acceptance is the word "smooth."

### Tech

- **[blocker] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18.** "Smooth" scrolling and "fast, no lag" loading, with no numeric bound, no device class, no frame floor. A 60fps Pixel 9 and a 12fps budget phone both pass. Also flagged by craft below as a plain-language defect. **Broken because:** nothing measurable means nothing testable, the story is Not-Testable per INVEST, and the should-label makes it the one most likely to ship unverified. **Fix now:** anchor to a device class already in the spec, e.g. "Given a 100-message conversation, when the user scrolls continuously, then the list renders at >=60fps on a mid-range Android (Snapdragon 665-class)," and "first message appears within 1.5s on 4G."

- **[major] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21.** AC requires swipe-right-to-match on recommended profiles to beat a random control by 20%, via an A/B test at p < 0.05, embedded as the acceptance criterion of a horizon:now must story. That is a weeks-long live-traffic experiment masquerading as a sprint gate. **Broken because:** the story cannot be accepted at sprint end, it needs accumulated production traffic and a significance test, breaking Independent and Testable. Engineering can only "ship to be evaluated later," which hides quality debt. **Fix now:** split it. Keep the build story with deterministic AC ("offline on 10,000 historical swipe events, precision@5 beats the distance-only baseline by >=10%"), and spin a separate experiment epic that owns the A/B design, infra, significance tracking, and rollout call.

- **[major] PRD-AIREC-E1-S1:20.** AC captures "a match converting to sustained messaging" as a training signal, but that event is produced by PRD-CHAT and no event schema, API contract, or bus spec exists anywhere. PRD-CHAT is `next`, AIREC is `later`, so by build time the contract must already be stable, and it is not. **Broken because:** the AIREC team will invent an undocumented integration point, and if PRD-CHAT's "sustained" threshold (the 7-day threshold) ever shifts, AIREC's training data silently rots. Not Independent per INVEST. **Fix now:** add `depends_on: [PRD-CHAT]` and an AC pinning the contract: "Given a conversation hits the 7-day sustained threshold, when the chat service emits the event, then AIREC's signal store receives {user_pair_id, match_id, event_type: 'sustained_7d', timestamp} within 60s."

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20.** AC fires "when the transaction completes," which presupposes a working payment system, while PRD-PREMIUM's own Won't list (line 78) says "payment gateway integration is Won't this round." The AC tests a flow the same spec says won't be built. **Broken because:** a tester cannot exercise it, no gateway is in scope, so the story gets stubbed (defeating the AC) or blocks on an out-of-scope dependency, and the contradiction surfaces mid-sprint. **Fix now:** either bring the gateway into scope with its own story, or decouple the AC from the real flow: "Given the payment service emits a successful-payment event for user X / plan Y, when the entitlement service receives it, then the who-liked-me flag for X is set true within 5s," testable against a stubbed event.

- **[minor] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:19.** AC requires each suggested profile to match >=3 of 4 user-set hard criteria (age, distance, gender, serious-intent), but no story builds the preference-setting UI or storage. MATCH's Should mentions "basic filters" yet nothing implements them. **Broken because:** with no user-side input, the filter output cannot be validated, the team hardcodes defaults (violating the AC) or blocks mid-sprint. Not Independent per INVEST. **Fix now:** add a prerequisite story, "user sets hard preferences in settings; persisted server-side; returned in the user-profile API," then add `depends_on: [that story id]` to S2.

### Market

- **[major] BRD:26.** The competitor list names four apps and omits Facebook Dating, which Vietnamese market data puts at ~17% of usage, third behind Tinder and Zalo/messaging. It is free, pre-installed inside an app 70M+ Vietnamese open daily, zero switching cost. A product selling "real connections" has to explain why anyone installs a new app when a social graph they already trust is one tab away. **Broken because:** with no threat assessment for Facebook Dating, the BRD-G1 growth model (100k MAU) underrates the default-to-Facebook path P-URBAN and P-PROVINCE actually face, and the acquisition plan has no answer to a zero-cost incumbent. **Fix now:** add Facebook Dating (and Zalo's social-discovery surface) to `competitors:` with threat: high, then make PRD-MATCH and onboarding state a concrete switching reason (verified identity + national pool + conversation-quality) that Facebook Dating cannot match. _(source: vietcetera.com/en/the-top-five-dating-apps-in-vietnam)_

- **[major] VISION:45.** The whole differentiation claim, "quality of connection over number of matches," is positioning with no structural moat. The north-star (7-day sustained chat) is an internal measurement target, not a feature a user feels. Tinder can bolt a "7-day streak" badge in a two-week sprint; Bumble already tracks conversation health and shipped Opening Moves. "We measure success differently" is not a switching reason for someone who already has Tinder. **Broken because:** with no network effect, data advantage, cultural depth, or switching cost, the product is a feature-clone target the moment any metric is worth copying, and the spec underweights the one real edge it has (safety verification, ahead of COMP-HEN). **Fix now:** promote the verified-identity layer to the primary moat in the Value Proposition, pair it with the national-pool advantage for P-PROVINCE as the structural reason Tinder's city-first density fails that segment. Both need investment and compliance work, not a two-week sprint, so they actually defend. _(source: cnn.com/2024/04/30/tech/bumble-relaunch-men-make-first-move)_

### Craft

- **[blocker] bundle root.** The bundle declares `lang: en` but all 28 artifacts are Vietnamese. **Broken because:** any validator, translator, or LLM reads `lang: en`, expects English AC and prose, then fails or silently chews Vietnamese as if it were English, garbling everything downstream. **Fix now:** change the bundle root `"lang": "en"` to `"lang": "vi"` to match what is actually written.

- **[major] BRD:17, BRD:57.** The BRD-G2 title mixes programmer notation with prose, `20% người dùng hoạt động tạo >=1 match mỗi tuần`, while the body says `từ 1 trở lên`. **Broken because:** a non-technical PO sees `>=1` and cannot tell whether it is a typo or jargon, and the two phrasings raise doubt about whether the requirement is even the same across the doc. **Fix now:** standardize to natural Vietnamese, change the title to `20% người dùng hoạt động tạo từ 1 match trở lên mỗi tuần` to match line 57.

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS:38-42.** The "Lưu ý thẳng" paragraph packs the north-star definition, an inline PRD-CHAT reference, three frontmatter symbols (`scope: out, moscow: could, horizon: later`), and a conclusion into five run-together lines. **Broken because:** the PO has to re-read it several times to pull out what the north-star is, that this is out of scope, why, and what the MoSCoW labels mean. A wall of subordinate clauses buries the priority. **Fix now:** break it into a bulleted list: north-star / problem-to-prove-first (PRD-CHAT) / status (scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later).

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS:44.** Prose drops the raw ID `PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3` inline, assuming the PO can navigate ID grammar mid-read. **Broken because:** the PO either skips it (losing context) or stops reading to go hunt the ID. Plain language should not force spec navigation. **Fix now:** replace it with a descriptive phrase, "Story này đề xuất tổ chức," reference by description, not ID, inside prose.
