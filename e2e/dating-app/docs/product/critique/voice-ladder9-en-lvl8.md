# Critique: all  ·  level 8  ·  lenses: product, tech, market, craft

> Severity tally: blocker 4 · major 8 · minor 2

## Top 3: rewrite now

1. **[blocker][product] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18** -- you bolted a national concert-ticketing business onto a dating app: city line-ups, 4 tickets an order, 10-minute seat holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters. Whoever owns this spec does not think like a product person. The BRD names three buyer personas, all hired to find a sustained real connection (VISION:51), and the only goals on the board are MAU, weekly-match-rate, premium break-even (BRD-G3:56). A ticketing engine serves none of them. The gold-plating note in the file is a confession, not a defense, and a confession does not make the scope creep disappear. Cut PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 from the spec. Park the offline-community idea as one line in the vision doc, no AC, no story shape that invites estimation. (DEC-worthy.)

2. **[blocker][tech] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18** -- the safety story's two AC are "absolutely safe" and "fully protect the user." Not one actor, attack surface, or observable outcome between them. This whole spec does the same thing every time something hard shows up: it gets a feel-good adjective instead of a control. No test can assert "absolutely safe." "Given bad behavior, then system fully protects" passes for any implementation including doing nothing, so the story ships and the security holes stay invisible. On a trust-critical product, that is the worst place to hide debt. Replace both AC with falsifiable controls, e.g. "Given a message contains a phone-number pattern, when delivered, then a safety-tip overlay shows, the message is not blocked, and the event is logged for moderation"; "Given a user taps Block, then no further messages are delivered either way and the blocked user cannot start a new conversation." Route chat safety into PRD-SAFETY where the trust job already lives.

3. **[blocker][market] BRD:58** -- BRD-G3 promises break-even by Year 2, but there is no unit economics under it. No target ARPU, no Year-1 conversion rate, no cost-to-serve at 100k MAU. Industry benchmark is 5-10% MAU-to-paid (5,000-10,000 payers at your scale), and PRD-PREMIUM is `horizon: later` and admits it is me-too (PRD-PREMIUM:42). A break-even number you assert but never model is a hope with a date on it. Year 1 carries identity-verification infra, a realtime chat backend, and moderation cost at zero premium revenue. With no funding bridge in the spec, the team either cuts safety/chat corners to lower burn or misses break-even outright, and nobody finds out until it is too late to fix. Add a unit-economics section to brd.md (ARPU range in VND, end-of-Year-1 conversion %, cost-to-serve per MAU at 100k). If Year 1 is pre-revenue by design, say so and add a runway constraint so PRD owners know they operate under burn. (Source: https://www.businessofapps.com/data/dating-app-benchmarks/) (DEC-worthy.)

## By lens

### Product

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:71** -- AIREC's whole deliverable this round is one Could item: rank discovery profiles by a model score instead of distance. That capability is already promised by PRD-MATCH-E1-S2, a must/now/core-value story with real AC (PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:19), and the AIREC file concedes it is me-too against Tinder/Bumble (PRD-AIREC:41). Two artifacts now own "rank profiles better." At build time engineers cannot tell whether smart ordering lives in MATCH or AIREC, so it gets built twice or AIREC stays a permanent empty placeholder. Fold AIREC's ranking ambition into PRD-MATCH-E1-S2 as a later-cycle enhancement; delete PRD-AIREC or shrink it to a "data-collection for future model" note. Make S2 the single home for ranking quality.
- **[major] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18** -- (see Top 3 #2) same problem on the product side: AC ("absolutely safe") is an unfalsifiable wish that duplicates PRD-SAFETY's real scope and burns a should-slot on a vibe. An "absolutely safe" AC never marks done, so it blocks the chat epic forever or gets waved through as theatre, and it muddies ownership of trust. Delete it; route block/report-in-thread and content moderation into PRD-SAFETY as concrete behaviour with measurable AC.
- **[major] BRD-G3:58** -- (see Top 3 #3) one of three business goals, and its only vehicle PRD-PREMIUM is scope:in/should/later with an empty Must and no committed story (PRD-PREMIUM:13). The riskiest belief in the spec is "people will pay," and nothing in the horizon tests it. If "will Vietnamese users pay for see-who-liked-you / boost" turns out to be wrong, the whole break-even goal collapses, and you find out in Year 2 with no time to course-correct. Pull one cheap willingness-to-pay probe into now/next (a paywall-intent read on the see-who-liked-you surface) and name the willingness-to-pay belief as the riskiest assumption in PRD-PREMIUM. (DEC-worthy.)
- **[minor] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18** -- a should-slot spent on "smooth, pleasant messaging" with AC "must be smooth" and "displays fast without lag," while the must-be reliability story (S1) carries the actual core-value load. Polishing a delighter before "smooth" is even definable burns effort on feel while reliable delivery, the thing that moves 7-day retention, stays the real driver. Vague AC cannot be accepted anyway. Fold the smoothness into S1's non-functional criteria with concrete thresholds (frame time, scroll-jank budget, load p95) tied to the retention metric, or drop it to could.

### Tech

- **[blocker] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18** -- "smooth" scrolling and "fast, not laggy" loading, no numeric bound, no device class, no OS, no list size, no frame floor. A Pixel 9 at 60fps and a low-end phone at 12fps both satisfy this. There is nothing to evaluate against, so the story cannot be tested deterministically. Not-Testable per INVEST, and the "should" label makes it likely to ship unverified. Rewrite: "Given a conversation of 100 messages, when scrolling continuously, then the list renders at 60fps or above on a mid-range Android (Snapdragon 665-class)"; "Given a conversation opens, when 50 messages load, then the first message appears within 1.5s on 4G."
- **[major] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21** -- the AC embeds a +20% swipe-to-match lift over control via A/B test at p<0.05, inside a horizon:now, moscow:must story. That is a weeks-long live-traffic experiment pretending to be a sprint-close criterion. The story cannot be accepted at sprint end; it needs production volume, A/B infra the spec never defines, and days of data. "Shipped to be evaluated later" is not "done," it is hidden quality debt. Split it. The build story keeps deterministic AC ("Given 10,000 historical swipe events, when the ranker runs offline, then precision@5 beats the distance-only baseline by at least 10%"); a separate experiment epic owns the A/B design, infra, significance, and rollout call.
- **[major] PRD-AIREC-E1-S1:20** -- the AC consumes a "match converting to sustained messaging" training event produced by PRD-CHAT, but no event schema, API contract, or bus spec exists anywhere. CHAT is `next`, AIREC is `later`. The AIREC team will invent an undocumented integration point, and if CHAT's definition of "sustained" (the 7-day threshold) shifts, AIREC's training data silently rots. Not Independent per INVEST. Add `depends_on: [PRD-CHAT]` and an AC that pins the contract: "Given a conversation hits the 7-day threshold, when the chat service emits the event, then AIREC's signal store receives {user_pair_id, match_id, event_type:'sustained_7d', timestamp} within 60s."
- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20** -- AC fires "when the transaction completes," but PRD-PREMIUM's Won't list (line 78) puts payment-gateway integration explicitly out of scope. The story tests a flow the same spec says will not be built. A tester cannot exercise this AC; it gets stubbed (defeating the AC) or blocked on an out-of-scope dependency, and the contradiction surfaces mid-sprint. Either move payment-gateway into scope with its own story, or decouple the precondition: "Given the payment service emits a successful-payment event for user X / plan Y, when the entitlement service receives it, then the who-liked-me flag for X is set true within 5s." (DEC-worthy: contradicts the artifact's own Won't clause.)
- **[minor] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:19** -- the 3-of-4 hard-criteria filter assumes a user preference-setting UI and stored profile that no story builds; MATCH's "basic filters" Should has no implementing story. With no preference input there is nothing to validate the filter output against; the team hardcodes defaults (violating the AC) or blocks mid-sprint. Not Independent. Add a prerequisite story ("user can set age range, max distance, desired gender, serious-intent toggle; persisted server-side, returned by the profile API") and add `depends_on: [that story]` to S2.

### Market

- **[major] BRD:26** -- the competitor list names four apps and omits Facebook Dating, roughly 17% of Vietnamese usage, free, pre-installed inside an app 70M+ Vietnamese open daily, zero switching cost. A product winning on "real connections" has to explain why anyone installs a new app when a trusted social graph is one tab away. With no threat assessment for that zero-cost incumbent, the BRD-G1 (100k MAU) growth model has no answer to the option P-URBAN and P-PROVINCE already have on their phones. Add Facebook Dating (and Zalo's discovery surface) to `competitors:` with threat: high, then make PRD-MATCH and onboarding articulate a concrete switching reason (verified identity + national pool + conversation quality) that Facebook Dating cannot match. (Source: https://vietcetera.com/en/the-top-five-dating-apps-in-vietnam)
- **[major] VISION:45** -- "quality of connection over number of matches" is a positioning line with no structural moat. The 7-day sustained-chat north-star is an internal metric, not something the user feels; Tinder can ship a "7-day streak" badge in a sprint and Bumble already promotes sustained engagement via Opening Moves. With no network effect, data advantage, cultural depth, or switching cost, the product is a feature-clone target the moment any metric is worth copying. The spec's real differentiators (verified identity ahead of COMP-HEN, national pool for P-PROVINCE) sit buried in risk tables. Promote verified identity to the primary moat in the Value Proposition, pair it with the national-pool advantage for P-PROVINCE against Tinder's city-first density, and name the mechanism, not the aspiration. (Source: https://www.cnn.com/2024/04/30/tech/bumble-relaunch-men-make-first-move) (DEC-worthy: a positioning ruling the PO should record.)

### Craft

- **[blocker] bundle root** -- the bundle declares `lang: en` but all 28 artifacts are Vietnamese. One field that tells every downstream tool what language to expect, and it is wrong. A validator, translator, or LLM reads `lang: en`, expects English AC and narrative, then fails or silently mangles Vietnamese as English downstream. Change bundle root `"lang": "en"` to `"lang": "vi"` to match what was actually authored.
- **[major] BRD:17, BRD:57** -- the BRD-G2 title mixes programmer notation `>=1` with Vietnamese prose while the body uses `từ 1 trở lên`. You jump between symbols and words for the same requirement. A non-technical PO sees `>=1` and wonders if it is a typo or jargon, and the prose variants raise doubt about whether the requirement is the same across the document, doubt without any added precision. Standardize to natural Vietnamese: change the BRD-G2 title to `20% người dùng hoạt động tạo từ 1 match trở lên mỗi tuần` to match line 57.
- **[major] PRD-EVENTS:38-42** -- the "Lưu ý thẳng" paragraph packs the north-star definition, an inline PRD-CHAT reference, three frontmatter symbols (`scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later`), and a conclusion into one wall of subordinate clauses. The PO must re-read it several times to extract what the north-star is, that the feature is out of scope, why, and what the MoSCoW labels mean. Break it into a bulleted list: **Why this is out of scope:** north-star = cặp duy trì nhắn tin 7 ngày (không phải sự kiện) · vấn đề chứng minh trước = "match rồi nhưng không trò chuyện" (PRD-CHAT) · status = scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later.
- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS:44** -- the prose drops the bare ID `PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3` inline, assuming the PO can parse ID grammar and navigate the spec mid-read. The PO either skips the ID and loses context or stops reading to look it up. Replace `PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 đề xuất tổ chức` with a descriptive phrase like `Một story cụ thể đề xuất tổ chức`, reference by description, not ID, inside prose.
