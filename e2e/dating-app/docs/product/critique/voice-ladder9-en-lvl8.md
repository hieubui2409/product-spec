## Top 3: fix now

1. **[blocker][market] BRD:42** -- Fika is filed as "low threat" while it sits at #1 on Google Play Vietnam, 750k+ downloads, $1.6M seed, AI matching, female-safety positioning, and a Vietnamese cultural story. Whoever wrote this map looked at the only funded local rival and decided it didn't matter. Everything Ghep Doi Viet claims for itself -- local, safe, meaningful -- Fika shipped first. **Trashed because:** the whole growth and differentiation plan stands on a false baseline. There is no open lane; Fika already owns that exact story plus a two-year data lead for any ranking model you bolt on later. **Rewrite it now:** upgrade COMP-FIKA to `high`, and add a section that names one thing you do that Fika does not. National reach for P-PROVINCE is the obvious candidate, and it belongs in an MVP story, not buried as a filter in PRD-MATCH. (`DEC-worthy` -- contradicts the approved competitor classification.) [source: saigoneer.com/.../fika-secures-1-6m-seed]

2. **[blocker][product+tech] PRD-CHAT-E1-S4:18** -- "Nhan tin an toan tuyet doi." Both AC are "the conversation must be absolutely safe" and "the system must fully protect the user." Two lenses caught the same thing: product says it is a hollow JTBD already owned by PRD-SAFETY; tech says it is un-testable by construction, yet it carries `scope: core-value, moscow: should`. This is a wish dressed as a story, and the spec keeps doing this -- vague where a number belongs. **Trashed because:** no test can ever be written against "tuyet doi," so the story loops forever in review or the build team invents its own safety surface and gold-plates an undefined one, on top of work PRD-SAFETY already does. **Rewrite it now:** delete the story and fold any real intent into PRD-SAFETY as concrete, testable behaviors. Block-and-report removes the sender's messages within 10s and queues the account; a harassment-keyword match holds the message pending review. Each behavior gets its own named AC.

3. **[blocker][tech] PRD-MATCH-E1-S2:21** -- One AC makes a statistically significant A/B result (right-swipe-to-match lift >=20%, p<0.05) a delivery condition for a `size:L, moscow:must, horizon:now` sprint story. Honestly, reading this is what wears me out: someone wrote a multi-week experiment into a merge gate and called it done-able. **Trashed because:** A/B significance needs live traffic and weeks of runtime, neither of which engineering controls at merge. The story can never formally close, and it blocks every downstream story that leans on recommendation quality. **Rewrite it now:** split it. Story one ships the ranking logic, cold-start fallback, and p95 load time (those AC are already testable, keep them). Story two (`horizon:next, moscow:should`) sets up and instruments the experiment with split-assignment logging. Move the 20%/p<0.05 target to the PRD success metric, not the AC.

## By lens

### Product

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:18** -- A full nationwide concert-ticketing operation -- artist line-ups, ticket tiers, 4-per-order holds, QR e-tickets, genre filters -- lives inside a dating app, serving no pain or gain named in VISION or BRD. The spec even admits this is solution-first "at a severe level" (PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3:45) and then specs five concrete AC anyway. **Trashed because:** artist relations, payments, and crowd safety are a separate company. Every hour spent here is an hour stolen from turning matches into 7-day conversations, which is the one thing this product claims to do better. **Rewrite it now:** cut the story entirely, or collapse it to a one-line "later vision" note in PRD-EVENTS with zero AC, so the backlog stops signalling a half-commitment. (`DEC-worthy`.)

- **[major] PRD-EVENTS:36** -- PRD-EVENTS is branded a "flagship feature" while carrying `scope:out, moscow:could, horizon:later`. Nobody flags their flagship as out-of-scope; that is two stories told at once. No persona is hiring this product for offline events, and the PRD itself concedes P-PROVINCE and P-RETURNEE are the worst-served by it (PRD-EVENTS:51-52). **Trashed because:** if anyone reads "flagship" literally, real engineering and field-ops budget gets pulled toward a feature serving none of the three personas, away from the trust and chat core the BRD calls the prerequisite for growth. **Rewrite it now:** pick one framing. Drop "flagship" and keep it an explicit parked experiment, or send it through real opportunity validation (which persona, which pain, which metric beyond the indirect MAU proxy) before it earns that word. (`DEC-worthy`.)

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:14** -- BRD-G3 says premium breaks even by year 2, yet the whole PRD is `horizon:later` and the riskiest belief is named then deferred (PRD-PREMIUM:22-24): that connection-first users will pay for Tinder-style boosts and see-who-liked-you. The single assumption that decides whether this is a business is scheduled to be tested last. **Trashed because:** if that belief is wrong, BRD-G3 is unreachable and there is no path to break-even. Discovering broken willingness-to-pay at the latest possible moment is how runway-funded products die. **Rewrite it now:** run an early cheap probe -- a price test or fake-door on "see who liked you" -- alongside the core in year 1. Record the kill/continue threshold and treat conversion as a riskiest-assumption test, not a year-2 surprise. (`DEC-worthy`.)

- **[minor] PRD-AIREC:88** -- AIREC maps itself to BRD-G2 (weekly-match-rate) while admitting it "almost certainly produces worse recommendations than competitors" until real interaction data exists (PRD-AIREC:43-44). The impact claim rests on data the product does not have. **Trashed because:** if AIREC gets pulled forward on that mapping, it ships a recommender worse than the distance default and optimizes raw matches over lasting conversation, actively undermining the 7-day metric. **Rewrite it now:** keep the could/later slot but strip the BRD-G2 claim until the core has logged interaction data, and restate success as "beats the distance-default on match-to-sustained-chat rate," not on match count.

### Tech

- **[blocker] PRD-PREMIUM-E1-S1:20** -- "Unlock within 5 seconds of completed transaction" assumes a payment can complete, but PRD-PREMIUM declares payment gateway integration Won't this round (line 78). The AC fires on an event no in-scope system can generate. **Trashed because:** with no gateway in scope there is no testable trigger. The story either blocks on an explicitly out-of-scope dependency or ships a stub that cannot mirror production -- and that is the revenue path (BRD-G3) untested from day one. **Rewrite it now:** add `depends_on: [PRD-PAYMENT]` to the frontmatter and mark the story blocked, or reframe the AC onto a testable internal event ("when the server confirms a successful payment webhook, the entitlement record is written and the UI unlocks within 5s") and document that it cannot be tested end-to-end until a gateway exists.

- **[major] PRD-CHAT-E1-S3:18** -- Both AC lean on "muon ma" (smooth) and "khong giat lag" (no lag) with no number for frame rate, keyboard response, or render time. Vague where a number belongs, again. **Trashed because:** QA cannot write a pass/fail test. One engineer's 20fps is another's "laggy," so the story is signed off by gut or stalls in review. **Rewrite it now:** anchor to a device class. 50 messages on a mid-range Android (Galaxy A-series), 5s continuous scroll stays >=55fps; conversation screen renders the first 20 messages within 1.5s on 4G. Both are automatable with Espresso/XCUITest profiling.

- **[major] PRD-SAFETY:75** -- The only data NFR for the liveness/ID flow is "processed safely, not visible to other users." No PDPD Decree 13/2023 frame for biometric and government-ID data, no residency rule, no third-party vendor vetting (the vendor receives raw biometrics), no deletion-audit beyond the 24h AC. **Trashed because:** collecting CCCD/passport images and facial biometrics with no documented legal basis is real regulatory and reputational exposure. The build team will pick the cheapest liveness provider rather than the compliant one, with zero spec guidance. **Rewrite it now:** add an NFR section -- data residency within jurisdiction, vendor must meet PDPD-equivalent obligations, a deletion log verifiable by compliance, and explicit informed consent before biometric capture.

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS-E1-S2:18** -- The seat-reservation AC ("press Giu cho, record the registration, decrement remaining by 1") has no concurrency or atomic-transaction constraint and no last-seat race handling. **Trashed because:** a read-then-write implementation double-books the last seat under load. The AC passes single-user and fails in production. **Rewrite it now:** add the AC "two simultaneous Giu cho for an event with exactly 1 seat left results in exactly one confirmation, the other gets a full-event error, count never below 0," plus an NFR requiring atomic decrement via DB transaction or optimistic locking.

### Market

- **[major] VISION:45** -- The entire moat is "we measure 7-day chat retention, not matches." That is a metric choice, not a feature. Hinge ran "designed to be deleted" years ago; Bumble built its brand on meaningful connection. Nothing here -- the swipe deck, the basic chat, the identity badge -- is architecturally harder to copy than a tagline, and the spec admits PRD-MATCH and PRD-CHAT sit at parity or behind. **Trashed because:** a positioning line with no execution moat evaporates the day a better-funded rival changes their slogan. P-URBAN, the paying persona, already has Tinder and Bumble installed and "quality over quantity" is a line both already use. **Rewrite it now:** elevate one structural moat from footnote to core. The P-PROVINCE national pool that Tinder's location-radius model underserves, or the liveness ID-verification trust layer (ahead of COMP-HEN), each deserves its own north-star metric.

- **[blocker] BRD:22** -- BRD-G3 targets year-2 break-even on premium with no price point, no conversion rate, no ARPU floor. Vietnam's online-dating market is roughly USD 6.4M/year (Statista). At the ~USD 8.15/year market ARPU, 100k MAU at 5% premium conversion is ~USD 40k/year -- one junior engineer in Hanoi, not break-even. **Trashed because:** a revenue goal with no price-times-conversion model is a wish. If serving 100k MAU (infra + moderation + ID-verification ops) runs USD 300-500k/year, the math only closes at 15-20% conversion, triple Tinder's benchmark, and the spec never confronts that arithmetic. **Rewrite it now:** add a unit-economics section to the BRD -- estimated operating cost at 100k MAU, the ARPU needed to cover it, the conversion that implies -- and if it doesn't close at Vietnamese willingness-to-pay, add a second monetization pillar before year 2. (`DEC-worthy`.) [source: statista.com/.../online-dating/vietnam]

### Craft

- **[major] VISION:27-28** -- "Ket noi that su duoc bat dau va duy tri" floats free while the north-star at VISION:51-52 is narrower ("cap doi duy tri nhan tin... tu 7 ngay tro len"); later text swaps the two terms in and out with no anchor. **Trashed because:** when "real connection" is vague and then operationalized as "7-day messaging," the team builds for message volume instead of relational depth. **Rewrite it now:** fix one canonical definition early in VISION ("Ket noi that = cap doi duy tri nhan tin qua lai lien tuc tu 7 ngay tro len trong thang") and use that exact phrasing in every downstream goal.

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:82-84** -- "Cach xep mac dinh hop ly" hides what the fallback actually is -- distance-only? distance plus activity? random? The word is evaluative, not prescriptive. **Trashed because:** a builder cannot implement "reasonable," so the fallback gets guessed and drifts silently from intent. **Rewrite it now:** state the rule -- "xep mac dinh theo khoang cach tang dan, roi theo do hoat dong giam dan."

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:16-26** -- The deferral rationale is a wall of nested clauses with "du du lieu tuong tac that" never quantified, so nobody can say when to stop deferring. **Trashed because:** the gate stays vague forever; no one can declare "threshold hit, build AI rec now." **Rewrite it now:** add a measurable gate -- "GATE TO START: PRD-MATCH + PRD-CHAT deliver 7+ day sustained-conversation data for 20%+ of MAU cohorts over 1 month."

- **[major] PRD-PREMIUM:82-85** -- "Han muc mien phi phai du rong... con dung duoc" -- "du rong" and "con dung duoc" are adjectives with no threshold. 10 likes/day? 100? **Trashed because:** product and engineering argue forever over what "wide enough" means and ship something misaligned. **Rewrite it now:** "Daily free swipe limit: minimum 20/day; Premium removes the limit."

- **[major] PRD-AIREC:38** -- "Co kha nang dan toi ket noi phu hop hon" stacks two vague comparatives with no threshold or definition of "suitable." **Trashed because:** the ranking-suitability rubric is never operationalized, so the model goal is a guess and you cannot verify the feature works. **Rewrite it now:** "co kha nang dan toi match chuyen thanh nhan tin duy tri >=7 ngay cao hon so voi xep theo khoang cach," or link to a numeric success metric.

- **[minor] PRD-PREMIUM:50-52** -- Three distinct persona value props (P-URBAN revenue, P-PROVINCE pool-scarcity, P-RETURNEE time-window) crammed into one run-on paragraph. **Trashed because:** the dense prose risks the builder missing that P-URBAN is the primary revenue driver and optimizing boost for the wrong persona. **Rewrite it now:** split into three one-sentence bullets, one motivation each.

- **[minor] PRD-EVENTS:41-48** -- The "Luu y lech trong tam" section reads like a PO journal entry dropped into the spec -- rhetorical "gold-plating," sarcasm about "van hanh nghe si, ban ve, an ninh dam dong." Editorial color where the build team needs a requirement. **Trashed because:** the in/out boundary gets muddied. A builder asks whether the sarcasm means skip it or just flag it. **Rewrite it now:** move the note to a decision log (`DEC-n`) or team notes; keep only the facts in the spec body -- "PRD-EVENTS-E1-S3 is scope:out/moscow:could/horizon:later; concert ticketing is an orthogonal domain; revisit after PRD-CHAT validates north-star."
